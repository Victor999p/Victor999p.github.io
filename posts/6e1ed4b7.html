🧠 BME 3102 Quantitative Human Physiology Comprehensive Notes

📝 Course Overview and Structure

  • Textbooks:
    • Boron & Boulpaep, Medical Physiology (2003, 3rd Edition 2016)
  • Course Description:
    • Quantitative, engineering approach to mammalian physiology.
    • Covers major human physiological systems: nervous, renal, cardiovascular, respiratory.
  • Primary Objective:
    • Understand physiology of major systems biologically and quantitatively.
    • Use mathematical models and engineering analysis.

🧠 Nervous System Physiology

Neuron Structure and Function

  • Components: (神經元結構)
    • Dendrites: receive signals (many to one) | 樹突：接收信號
    • Soma (cell body) | 胞體（細胞體）
    • Axon hillock: integrates signals | 軸初段：整合信號
    • Axon: transmits signals (one to many) | 軸突：傳遞信號
    • Presynaptic terminal (bouton): releases neurotransmitters | 突前膜末梢：釋放神經遞質
  • Central Nervous System (CNS): brain and spinal cord | 中樞神經系統：大腦和脊髓
  • Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): 12 pairs cranial nerves, 31 pairs spinal nerves | 周圍神經系統：12對腦神經，31對脊神經

🩺 Renal Physiology

Main Functions

  • Maintain internal fluid environment. | 維持內部液體環境。

  • Urine formation: | 尿液形成：

    • Filter metabolic products. | 過濾代謝產物。

    • Regulate fluid and electrolyte balance. | 調節液體和電解質平衡。

    • Regulate blood pressure and volume. | 調節血壓和體積。

Anatomy

  • Location: Lower back, between T12 & L3 vertebrae. | 位置：下背部，在T12和L3椎骨之間。

  • Size: ~10 cm long, 5 cm wide, 4 cm thick. | 大小：約10厘米長，5厘米寬，4厘米厚。

  • Blood flow: ~20% cardiac output (~1 L/min). | 血流量：約20%心輸出（約1升/分）。

  • Kidney receives massive blood flow despite small mass (~0.5% body weight). | 腎臟儘管質量小（約0.5%體重），但接收大量血流量。

Conservation of Mass

textArterialFlow=textVenousFlow+textUrineOutput\\text{Arterial Flow} = \\text{Venous Flow} + \\text{Urine Output}textArterialFlow=textVenousFlow+textUrineOutput

Filtration and Selectivity

  • Filters ~180 L plasma/day (~60x plasma volume). | 每天過濾約180升血漿（約60倍血漿體積）。

  • Highly selective: only ~0.17% of plasma volume excreted as urine. | 高度選擇性：只有約0.17%的血漿體積以尿液形式排出。

  • Filtration barrier: | 過濾屏障：

    • Fenestrated capillaries. | 窗孔毛細血管。

    • Basement membrane (negatively charged). | 基底膜（負電荷）。

    • Podocyte filtration slits. | 足細胞過濾縫隙。

  • Size and charge affect filtration: | 大小和電荷影響過濾：

    • <1.5 nm freely filtered. | <1.5 nm自由過濾。

    • 4.2 nm not filtered. | 4.2 nm不被過濾。

    • Anions filtered less due to negative charge. | 陰離子由於負電荷過濾較少。

Models of Selectivity

| Model | Description | 描述 |

| — | — | — |

| 1 | Selective secretion via transporters, channels, exchangers; active removal from blood. | 通過轉運體、通道、交換器的選擇性分泌；從血液中主動移除。 |

| 2 | Passive filter followed by selective resorption via transporters and exchangers. | 被動過濾後通過轉運體和交換器的選擇性重吸收。 |

| 3 | Combination of passive filtration and active removal/resorption. | 被動過濾和主動移除/重吸收的組合。 |

Nephron Structure and Function

  • Functional unit: ~1 million nephrons/kidney. | 功能單位：每腎約100萬個腎單位。

  • Components: | 組件：

    • Glomerulus: leaky capillary, filters plasma. | 腎小球：漏毛細血管，過濾血漿。

    • Bowman’s capsule: collects filtrate. | 鮑曼囊：收集濾液。

    • Tubule: active excretion and resorption. | 小管：主動排泄和重吸收。

    • Peritubular capillaries: resorb solutes and water. | 周圍毛細血管：重吸收溶質和水。

    • Loop of Henle: generates osmotic gradient for urine concentration. | 亨利環：產生尿液濃縮的滲透梯度。

    • Collecting duct: fine control of ion and water balance. | 集合管：對離子和水平衡的精細控制。

Regulation

  • Juxtaglomerular apparatus senses NaCl concentration. | 球旁器感測NaCl濃度。

  • Baroreceptors in afferent arteriole sense pressure. | 輸入小動脈中的壓力感受器感測壓力。

  • Renin release leads to angiotensin II production, vasoconstriction, and blood pressure regulation. | 腎素釋放導致血管緊張素II產生，血管收縮，和血壓調節。

  • Sympathetic nervous system modulates renal blood flow. | 交感神經系統調節腎血流量。

  • Clearance: volume of plasma cleared of a substance per unit time.

  • Mass balance equation:

  • Inulin clearance measures glomerular filtration rate (GFR).

  • Fractional excretion (FE):

Glomerular Filtration

  • Driven by net Starling forces:

    PUF=(PGC−PBS)−(piGC−piBS)P_{UF} = (P_{GC} - P_{BS}) - (\\pi_{GC} - \\pi_{BS})PUF​=(PGC​−PBS​)−(piGC​−piBS​)

  • GFR proportional to ultrafiltration coefficient (K_f) and (P_{UF}).

  • Autoregulation maintains constant glomerular hydrostatic pressure despite changes in arterial pressure.

  • Afferent arteriole constriction decreases GFR; efferent arteriole constriction increases GFR.

Urine Concentration Mechanism

  • Countercurrent multiplier in Loop of Henle creates hyperosmotic medullary gradient.
  • Differential permeability of tubule segments:
    • Proximal straight tubule (PST): permeable to water and NaCl.
    • Thin descending limb (tDLH): permeable to water, low NaCl permeability.
    • Thin ascending limb (tALH): low water permeability, permeable to NaCl.
    • Thick ascending limb (TAL): low water permeability, actively pumps NaCl out.
  • Collecting ducts pass through hyperosmotic medulla; permeability regulated by arginine vasopressin (AVP/ADH).
  • Surfactant reduces surface tension in alveoli, increasing lung compliance.

❤️ Cardiovascular Physiology

Major Functions

  • Respiration: transport O₂ and CO₂.
  • Nutrition: supply nutrients.
  • Excretion: remove metabolic waste.
  • Hydration maintenance.
  • Temperature regulation.
  • Hormone delivery.
  • Immune protection.

Anatomy

  • Heart: two pumps in series.
    • Right heart: pumps deoxygenated blood to lungs.
    • Left heart: pumps oxygenated blood to body.
  • Four chambers: atria (priming pumps), ventricles (high pressure pumps).
  • Valves prevent backflow: tricuspid, pulmonary, mitral, aortic.
  • Blood vessels: arteries, arterioles, capillaries, venules, veins.

Hemodynamics

  • Blood flow modeled as laminar flow in rigid tubes.

  • Flow rate (Q = V \times A).

  • Bernoulli’s equation relates pressure and velocity.

  • Vascular resistance:

    R=frac8muLpia4R = \\frac{8 \\mu L}{\\pi a^4}R=frac8muLpia4

  • Compliance measures vessel stretchability:

    Cu=frac2pir3EhC_u = \\frac{2 \\pi r^3}{E h}Cu​=frac2pir3Eh

    where (E) is Young’s modulus, (h) wall thickness.

Cardiac Electrophysiology

  • Pacemaker cells: SA node (~60 bpm), AV node (~40 bpm), Purkinje fibers (~20 bpm).
  • Myocytes electrically coupled via gap junctions.
  • Cardiac action potential phases:
    • Phase 0: rapid Na⁺ influx (depolarization).
    • Phase 1: Na⁺ channel inactivation, transient K⁺ current.
    • Phase 2: Ca²⁺ influx plateau.
    • Phase 3: K⁺ efflux (repolarization).
    • Phase 4: resting potential.
  • ECG measures electrical activity via limb and precordial leads.
  • Einthoven’s triangle defines limb leads I, II, III.
  • Augmented unipolar leads (aVR, aVL, aVF) and precordial leads (V1-V6) provide spatial information.

Cardiac Cycle

  • Four phases:
    1. Filling (inflow): AV valves open, ventricles fill.
    2. Isovolumic contraction: valves closed, pressure rises.
    3. Ejection (outflow): semilunar valves open, blood ejected.
    4. Isovolumic relaxation: valves closed, ventricles relax.
  • Stroke volume (SV) = End diastolic volume (EDV) - End systolic volume (ESV).
  • Cardiac output (CO) = SV × heart rate (f).
  • Pressure-volume (PV) loops illustrate cardiac function.
  • Starling’s law: increased preload increases SV and CO.
  • Afterload increase reduces SV.

Cardiac Muscle Mechanics

  • Sarcomere: basic contractile unit.
  • Force-length relationship:
    • Active tension peaks at optimal sarcomere length (~2.0 µm).
    • Passive tension increases with stretch.
  • Cardiac muscle has more passive tension than skeletal muscle.
  • Compliance changes between diastole (high compliance) and systole (low compliance).

Cardiac Output Model

  • Two-state spring model for ventricle compliance.

  • CO depends on preload (P_f), afterload (P_a), contractility, and heart rate.

  • Combined heart and peripheral circulation model predicts CO and venous return.

  • Mean systemic pressure (P_{ms}) is intrinsic to vascular system.

  • Venous return:

    Q=fracPms−PRARv+fracCaCa+CvRaQ = \\frac{P_{ms} - P_{RA}}{R_v + \\frac{C_a}{C_a + C_v} R_a}Q=fracPms​−PRA​Rv​+fracCa​Ca​+Cv​Ra​

  • Cardiac output and venous return curves intersect at operating point.

🌬 Respiratory Physiology

Anatomy and Function

  • Components: lungs, right heart, pulmonary circulation.
  • Functional unit: alveolus (~300 µm diameter).
  • Total alveolar surface area ~85 m².
  • Gas exchange: O₂ uptake, CO₂ removal.
  • Secondary functions: pH regulation, blood reserve, filtration.

Ventilation

  • Tidal volume ~500 ml, ~15 breaths/min → 7.5 L/min total ventilation.
  • Dead space ~150 ml (conducting airways without alveoli).
  • Alveolar ventilation ~5.25 L/min.
  • Spirometry measures lung volumes and capacities.
  • Helium dilution technique measures residual volume (RV) or functional residual capacity (FRC).
  • Dead space volume calculated using CO₂ concentration differences.

Lung Mechanics

  • Surface tension at alveolar air-liquid interface creates pressure barrier:

    DeltaP=frac2sigmar\\Delta P = \\frac{2 \\sigma}{r}DeltaP=frac2sigmar

  • Water surface tension ~72 dynes/cm; surfactant reduces to ~1 dyne/cm.

  • Surfactant increases lung compliance, reduces work of breathing.

  • Compliance affected by diseases (edema, pneumonia, emphysema).

Airflow Dynamics

  • Airflow can be laminar or turbulent.
  • Reynolds number determines flow type; laminar if <2000.
  • Upper airways have turbulent flow, generating sound.
  • Resistance depends on airway generation and cross-sectional area.

Ventilation Model

  • Lung modeled as RC circuit:

    • Resistance (R) to airflow.
    • Compliance (C) of lung volume.

  • Lung volume (V_l(t)) follows:

    Vl(t)=CPD(1−e−t/tau),quadtau=RCV_l(t) = C P_D (1 - e^{-t/\\tau}), \\quad \\tau = R CVl​(t)=CPD​(1−e−t/tau),quadtau=RC

  • Asthma increases (R), increasing (\tau), reducing ventilation efficiency.

Gas Transport and Diffusion

  • Governed by Fick’s Law:

    fracpartialNpartialt=−ADalphafracpartialPpartialx\\frac{\\partial N}{\\partial t} = - A D \\alpha \\frac{\\partial P}{\\partial x}fracpartialNpartialt=−ADalphafracpartialPpartialx

  • Partial pressure (P) relates to concentration (c) by solubility (\alpha):

    c=alphaPc = \\alpha Pc=alphaP

  • Diffusion time constant:

    tau=fracx22D\\tau = \\frac{x^2}{2D}tau=fracx22D

  • Typical (\tau \approx 4, ms), transit time ~1 s → sufficient for gas exchange.

Hemoglobin and Oxygen Transport

  • Hemoglobin (Hb) increases O₂ carrying capacity ~65x plasma alone.
  • Hb saturation curve is sigmoidal, cooperative binding.
  • Oxygen loading kinetics slower due to Hb binding, but transit time sufficient.
  • Transport modeled as two resistances in series: diffusion and Hb binding.

Lung Perfusion

  • Pulmonary circulation is low pressure.
  • Blood flow varies by lung zone due to hydrostatic pressure gradients.
  • Four zones defined by relative pressures of alveolar, arterial, and venous pressures.
  • Perfusion heterogeneity affects gas exchange efficiency.

Ventilation-Perfusion (V/Q) Matching

  • Normal V/Q ratio ~1.

  • V/Q mismatch leads to hypoxia or hypercapnia.

  • Alveolar and arterial partial pressures depend on V/Q ratio:

    PA,O2=fracPV,O2+PI,O2fracdotVAQ1+fracdotVAQ,quadPA,CO2=fracPV,CO21+fracdotVAQP_{A,O_2} = \\frac{P_{V,O_2} + P_{I,O_2} \\frac{\\dot{V}_A}{Q}}{1 + \\frac{\\dot{V}_A}{Q}}, \\quad P_{A,CO_2} = \\frac{P_{V,CO_2}}{1 + \\frac{\\dot{V}_A}{Q}}PA,O2​​=fracPV,O2​​+PI,O2​​fracdotVA​Q1+fracdotVA​Q,quadPA,CO2​​=fracPV,CO2​​1+fracdotVA​Q

  • Exercise increases ventilation and perfusion to meet metabolic demands.

  • CO₂ ventilation tightly regulated to maintain acid-base balance.

Summary

  • The course integrates quantitative and biological perspectives on human physiology.
  • Mathematical models describe electrical activity in neurons, renal filtration and clearance, cardiovascular dynamics, and respiratory gas exchange.
  • Understanding these models aids in interpreting physiological function and pathophysiology.
  • Emphasis on engineering principles such as circuit analogies, diffusion laws, and fluid dynamics.
  • Clinical relevance highlighted through ECG interpretation, renal clearance tests, and respiratory function assessments.

Important Vocabulary and Concepts

  • Membrane Potential: Voltage difference across a cell membrane. (Cantonese: 膜電位：細胞膜兩側的電位差。)
  • Membrane Capacitance (Cm): Ability of membrane to store charge. (Cantonese: 膜電容 (Cm)：膜層儲存電荷的能力。)
  • Membrane Resistance (Rm): Resistance to ion flow across membrane. (Cantonese: 膜電阻 (Rm)：膜層對離子流動的阻力。)
  • Nernst Potential: Equilibrium potential for a specific ion. (Cantonese: 能斯特電位：特定離子的平衡電位。)
  • Passive Membrane Model: Membrane modeled as an RC circuit for voltage dynamics. (Cantonese: 被動膜模型：將膜視為RC電路以描述電壓變化。)
  • Action Potential: Rapid change in membrane potential during neuron firing. (Cantonese: 動作電位：神經元放電時膜電位的快速變化。)
  • Hodgkin-Huxley Model: Describes ionic currents and action potentials. (Cantonese: 霍奇金-赫胥黎模型：描述離子電流和動作電位。)
  • Gating Variable: Probability of an ion channel gate being open. (Cantonese: 閘變數：離子通道閘開啟的概率。)
  • Cable Theory: Models electrical signal propagation in neurons. (Cantonese: 電纜理論：模擬神經元中電信號的傳播。)
  • Fick’s Law: Relates diffusive flux to concentration gradients. (Cantonese: 費克定律：將擴散通量與濃度梯度聯繫起來。)
  • Nephron: Functional unit of the kidney that filters blood and forms urine. (Cantonese: 腎元：腎臟中過濾血液並形成尿液的功能單位。)
  • Renal Clearance: Volume of plasma cleared of a substance per time. (Cantonese: 腎清除率：單位時間內清除物質的血漿體積。)
  • Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR): Rate of plasma filtration in kidneys. (Cantonese: 腎小球濾過率 (GFR)：腎臟中血漿濾過的速率。)
  • Starling Forces: Balance of hydrostatic and oncotic pressures driving filtration. (Cantonese: 斯塔林力：驅動濾過的靜水壓和滲透壓平衡。)
  • Countercurrent Multiplier: Mechanism in Loop of Henle for urine concentration. (Cantonese: 逆流倍增器：亨利環中尿液濃縮的機制。)
  • Renin-Angiotensin System: Hormonal system regulating blood pressure and volume. (Cantonese: 腎素-血管緊張素系統：調節血壓和血容量的激素系統。)
  • Autoregulation: Local control that maintains blood flow despite pressure changes. (Cantonese: 自我調節：在壓力變化下維持血流的局部控制。)
  • Surfactant: Reduces alveolar surface tension, increases lung compliance. (Cantonese: 表面活性劑：降低肺泡表面張力，增加肺順應性。)
  • Compliance: Measure of vessel or lung stretchability. (Cantonese: 順應性：血管或肺部伸展性的測量。)
  • Hemoglobin: Protein that carries oxygen in red blood cells. (Cantonese: 血紅蛋白：紅血球中運送氧氣的蛋白質。)
  • Stroke Volume (SV): Volume of blood ejected by the ventricle per beat. (Cantonese: 每搏輸出量 (SV)：每次心室收縮射出的血量。)
  • Cardiac Output (CO): Volume of blood pumped by the heart per minute. (Cantonese: 心輸出量 (CO)：心臟每分鐘泵出的血液體積。)
  • Mean Systemic Pressure (Pms): Pressure in the systemic circulation if flow were stopped. (Cantonese: 平均體循環壓力 (Pms)：若血流停止時體循環中的壓力。)
  • Ventilation-Perfusion Ratio (V/Q): Balance of air flow and blood flow in lungs. (Cantonese: 通風-灌注比 (V/Q)：肺部通氣與灌注的平衡。)
  • Respiratory Quotient (RQ): Ratio of CO₂ produced to O₂ consumed. (Cantonese: 呼吸商 (RQ)：產生的CO₂與消耗的O₂的比率。)
