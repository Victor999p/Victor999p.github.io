Topic 1 – Fibronectin 纖連蛋白 同 Integrin 整合素 Binding 結合

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Fibronectin 纖連蛋白係關鍵嘅 ECM (Extracellular Matrix) 細胞外基質 glycoprotein 糖蛋白，透過 integrins 整合素促進 cell adhesion 細胞黏附，對組織結構同信號傳遞至關重要。

思維導圖 (Mind map):
Fibronectin → Types 類型 (Soluble Plasma 可溶性血漿型 → Bloodstream 血液循環 → Hepatocytes 肝細胞; Insoluble Cellular 不可溶性細胞型 → Secreted by Fibroblasts 成纖維細胞分泌) → Binds to 結合對象 (Integrins 整合素 → RGD Sequence RGD序列 → α5β1/αVβ3; ECM Components ECM成分 → Collagen 膠原蛋白/Fibrin 纖維蛋白/Heparan Sulfate 硫酸肝素) → Enables Cell Attachment 實現細胞黏附。

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • 🔴 Fibronectin 纖連蛋白係結合 integrins 整合素嘅主要 ECM 糖蛋白。
  • 🟢 亦結合其他 ECM 成分，如 collagen 膠原蛋白、fibrin 纖維蛋白、heparan sulfate proteoglycans 硫酸肝素蛋白聚糖。
  • 🔴 兩種類型 (Two types): soluble plasma 可溶性血漿型 (hepatocytes 肝細胞產生) 同 insoluble cellular 不可溶性細胞型 (fibroblasts 成纖維細胞等分泌)。
  • 🟢 Integrin 整合素 binding 結合使用 RGD sequence RGD序列 (Arg-Gly-Asp 精氨酸-甘氨酸-天冬氨酸)。
  • 🔴 關鍵 integrins 整合素: α5β1 同 αVβ3 (喺細胞表面)。
  • 🟢 短嘅 RGD peptides RGD肽可以模仿 fibronectin 纖連蛋白功能，促進 cell attachment 細胞黏附。
  • 🔵 RGD 序列促成 focal adhesion 黏著斑形成。
  • 🟢 Plasma fibronectin 血漿纖連蛋白喺血液中循環；cellular fibronectin 細胞型纖連蛋白則整合入 matrix 基質。
  • 🔴 RGD 係 primary cell attachment site 主要細胞黏附位點。
  • 🟢 Binding 結合促進 clustering 聚集，形成 focal adhesions 黏著斑。
  • 🔵 應用於 biomaterials 生物材料，用於 cell adhesion engineering 細胞黏附工程。
  • 🟢 Insoluble form 不可溶性型由好多細胞分泌，例如 fibroblasts 成纖維細胞。
  • 🔴 Heparan sulfate 硫酸肝素 binding 結合有助於 growth factor 生長因子 localization 定位。
  • 🟢 Fibronectin 纖連蛋白 domains 結構域可以結合 TGF-β 同 bFGF。

常見錯誤同考官陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps)

  • 誤以為 RGD 係唯一結合位點 (陷阱: 忘記其他協同區域，例如 PHSRN)。
  • 混淆 fibronectin 纖連蛋白嘅類型: 以為所有纖連蛋白都係 insoluble 不可溶性，喺 ECM 入面。
  • 忽略 RGD 喺 tissue engineering (TE) 組織工程應用中，用於 synthetic peptides 合成肽嘅作用。

記憶法 (Mnemonics / Memory Hooks)

  • RGD: “Really Good Dance” – Arg-Gly-Asp 精氨酸-甘氨酸-天冬氨酸令細胞”跳舞” (黏附同移動)。
  • Fibronectin types 纖連蛋白類型: “Plasma Soluble in Blood, Cellular Insoluble in Matrix” – PSB-CIM。

過往試題 + 標準答案 (Past Exam Questions + Model Answers)

What is the role of the RGD sequence in fibronectin? 纖連蛋白中 RGD 序列嘅作用係咩？

The RGD (Arg-Gly-Asp) sequence is the primary integrin-binding site facilitating cell attachment via α5β1 and αVβ3 integrins.
RGD (精氨酸-甘氨酸-天冬氨酸) 序列係主要嘅整合素結合位點，透過 α5β1 同 αVβ3 整合素促進細胞黏附。

Compare soluble and insoluble fibronectin. 比較可溶性同不可溶性纖連蛋白。

Soluble plasma fibronectin circulates in blood from hepatocytes for systemic roles; insoluble cellular fibronectin is secreted locally for ECM integration and adhesion.
可溶性血漿型纖連蛋白由肝細胞產生，喺血液中循環，發揮全身性作用；不可溶性細胞型纖連蛋白由局部細胞分泌，用於 ECM 整合同黏附。

Topic 2 – Focal Adhesions 黏著斑

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Focal adhesions 黏著斑係 integrin 整合素介導嘅聚集結構，連結 ECM 細胞外基質 同 cytoskeleton 細胞骨架，傳遞信號同機械力。
思維導圖 (Mind map):
Focal Adhesion 黏著斑 → Integrin Binds 整合素結合 (Collagen 膠原蛋白/Fibronectin 纖連蛋白 → RGD) → Cluster Formation 聚集形成 → Components 組成部分 (Vinculin/Talin/FAK/Actin/Myosin) → Signals to Nucleus 信號傳至細胞核 → 2D (Punctate 點狀) vs 3D (Diffuse 瀰漫性).

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • 🔴 Integrins 整合素 bind 結合 ECM (如 collagen 膠原蛋白/fibronectin 纖連蛋白) 形成 focal adhesions 黏著斑。
  • 🟢 Focal adhesions 黏著斑係細胞黏附聚集結構。
  • 🔴 顯微成像中常用綠色螢光標記 focal adhesions 黏著斑。
  • 🟢 2D 環境下嘅 adhesions 黏著斑呈 punctate 點狀；3D 環境下呈 fibrillar/diffuse 纖維狀/瀰漫性。
  • 🔵 透過 cytoskeleton 細胞骨架將 mechanical signals 機械信號傳遞至 nucleus 細胞核。
  • 🟢 涉及蛋白質: vinculin, talin, paxillin, FAK。
  • 🔴 Neural progenitor cells (NPCs) 神經祖細胞: 喺 3D 環境中，remodeling 重塑 > binding 結合。
  • 🟢 NPC stemness NPC幹性對 3D 環境中嘅 adhesive ligands 黏附配體不敏感。
  • 🔵 應用於 TE 組織工程，用於模仿 ECM 嘅 scaffold 支架設計。
  • 🟢 機械力來自 actin-myosin contraction 肌動蛋白-肌球蛋白收縮。
  • 🔴 Turnover 更新調節 migration 遷移。
  • 🟢 FAK 透過 phosphorylation 磷酸化進行信號傳遞。
  • 🔵 3D 環境會改變 adhesion morphology 黏附形態。

常見錯誤同考官陷阱 (Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps)

  • 假設 2D 同 3D 嘅 adhesions 黏著斑係一樣嘅 (陷阱: 喺 TE 設計中忽略維度影響)。
  • 忘記 focal adhesions 黏著斑除咗黏附，仲有 signaling 信號傳遞嘅作用。
  • 忽略 NPC 神經祖細胞喺 3D 中對 ligands 配體不敏感呢點。

記憶法 (Mnemonics / Memory Hooks)

  • Focal Adhesion 黏著斑: “FACTS” – Fibronectin-Actin Connection Transmits Signals 纖連蛋白-肌動蛋白連接傳遞信號。
  • 2D vs 3D: “Punctate in Plane (2D), Fibrils in Full (3D)” 平面上點狀(2D)，立體中纖維(3D)。

過往試題 + 標準答案 (Past Exam Questions + Model Answers)

Describe focal adhesion formation. 描述黏著斑嘅形成。

Integrins bind ECM (e.g., fibronectin via RGD), clustering with talin/vinculin/actin to form adhesions transmitting signals.
整合素結合 ECM (例如，透過 RGD 結合纖連蛋白)，與 talin/vinculin/肌動蛋白聚集形成黏著斑，傳遞信號。

How do focal adhesions differ in 2D vs 3D? 黏著斑喺 2D 同 3D 環境中有咩唔同？

In 2D, they are punctate for strong anchorage; in 3D, fibrillar for remodeling, as seen in NPC maintenance.
喺 2D 環境，佢哋呈點狀用於強力錨定；喺 3D 環境，呈纖維狀用於重塑，正如喺 NPC 維持中所見。

Topic 3 – Cell Migration 細胞遷移 同 Chemotaxis 趨化性

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Cell migration 細胞遷移涉及 cytoskeleton 細胞骨架重組以進行移動，由 taxis 趨向性引導，例如 development 發育同 repair 修復中嘅 chemotaxis 趨化性。
Key Terms: Actin polymerization 肌動蛋白聚合, Myosin 肌球蛋白, Haptotaxis 趨觸性, Phototaxis 趨光性, Embryogenesis 胚胎發生。

Topic 4 – Signaling Ligands 信號配體 同 Types 類型

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Ligands 配體啟動 signaling 信號以協調組織發育，根據類型、作用範圍同功能分類。
Key Terms: Diffusible 可擴散, ECM-bound 細胞外基質結合, Endocrine 內分泌, Paracrine 旁分泌, Autocrine 自分泌, Juxtacrine 近分泌/並列分泌, Growth factors (GFs) 生長因子, Cytokines 細胞因子, Chemokines 趨化因子.

Topic 5 – Central Paradigm of Signal Transduction 信號轉導中心法則

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Signal transduction 信號轉導將 extracellular 細胞外 ligands 配體轉化為 intracellular 細胞內 responses 反應，透過 receptors 受體同 cascades 級聯反應，最終導致 gene activation 基因激活。
Key Terms: Initiation 啟動, Transduction 轉導, Transcription factors (TFs) 轉錄因子, Second messengers 第二信使, Translation 翻譯, mRNA vaccines mRNA疫苗。

Topic 6 – G-Protein Coupled Receptors (GPCRs) G蛋白偶聯受體

大圖景 (Big Picture)

GPCRs 透過 G-protein G蛋白解離傳遞多樣化信號，參與 sensory perception 感官知覺同生理調節。
Key Terms: 7 transmembrane (7TM) segments 7次跨膜片段, Agonist 激動劑, G-protein subunits (α/β/γ) G蛋白亞基, Effectors 效應器, Adenylate cyclase 腺苷酸環化酶, Phospholipase C (PLC) 磷脂酶C。

Topic 7 – Receptor Tyrosine Kinases (RTKs) 受體酪氨酸激酶 同 Pathways 信號途徑

大圖景 (Big Picture)

RTKs 喺 ligand 配體結合後 dimerize 二聚化並發生 phosphorylation 磷酸化，啟動 cascades 級聯反應，參與生長同 angiogenesis 血管生成。
Key Terms: Dimerization 二聚化, Autophosphorylation 自磷酸化, Ras/Raf/MEK/ERK pathway, TGF-β (Transforming Growth Factor-β) 轉化生長因子-β, Smad proteins Smad蛋白, Wnt pathway, β-catenin β-連環蛋白。

Topic 8 – Mechanotransduction 機械轉導 同 Cytoskeleton 細胞骨架

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Mechanotransduction 機械轉導將 physical forces 物理力轉化為 biochemical responses 生化反應，透過 cytoskeleton 細胞骨架實現，對骨同血管健康至關重要。
Key Terms: Shear stress 剪切應力, Tension 張力, Actin filaments (Microfilaments) 肌動蛋白絲 (微絲), Microtubules (MTs) 微管, Intermediate filaments (IFs) 中間絲, Treadmilling 踏車運動, Dynamic instability 動態不穩定性, Stress shielding 應力遮擋。

15-Minute Mock Exam 15分鐘模擬考試

  1. Explain the role of RGD in cell adhesion and its TE applications.

    RGD (Arg-Gly-Asp) is the integrin-binding site in fibronectin promoting attachment; in TE, short RGD peptides functionalize scaffolds for cell adhesion without full proteins.

  2. Compare focal adhesions in 2D vs 3D environments, with NPC example.

    2D: punctate, ligand-sensitive; 3D: fibrillar, remodeling-focused; for NPCs, 3D stemness maintenance is insensitive to ligands, prioritizing remodeling.

  3. Describe chemotaxis and its measurement.

    Directed migration to chemical gradients; assessed by polarized morphology and proportion of cells moving toward/away from attractant, depending on concentration and gradient steepness.

  4. Outline the central signal transduction paradigm.

    Initiation: ligand-receptor binding; Transduction: cascade activates nuclear TF; Gene activation: TF binds DNA, leading to transcription, translation, and physiological changes.

  5. How does Wnt canonical pathway work?

    Ligand binds Fz/LRP, stabilizes β-catenin, which enters nucleus and interacts with TCF/Lef for gene transcription in development and cancer.

Topic 1 – Fibronectin and Integrin Binding

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Fibronectin is a key ECM glycoprotein facilitating cell adhesion via integrins, crucial for tissue structure and signaling.
Mind map: Fibronectin → Types (Soluble Plasma → Bloodstream → Hepatocytes; Insoluble Cellular → Secreted by Fibroblasts) → Binds to (Integrins → RGD Sequence → α5β1/αVβ3; ECM Components → Collagen/Fibrin/Heparan Sulfate) → Enables Cell Attachment.

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Fibronectin is the major ECM glycoprotein binding integrins.
  • 🟢 Binds other ECM like collagen, fibrin, heparan sulfate proteoglycans.
  • 🔴 Two types: soluble plasma (hepatocytes) and insoluble cellular (fibroblasts).
  • 🟢 Integrin binding uses RGD sequence (Arg-Gly-Asp).
  • 🔴 Key integrins: α5β1 and αVβ3 on cell surface.
  • 🟢 Short RGD peptides can mimic fibronectin for cell attachment.
  • 🔵 RGD enables focal adhesion formation.
  • 🟢 Plasma fibronectin circulates in blood; cellular is matrix-integrated.
  • 🔴 RGD is the primary cell attachment site.
  • 🟢 Binding promotes clustering into focal adhesions.
  • 🔵 Used in biomaterials for cell adhesion engineering.
  • 🟢 Insoluble form secreted by many cells like fibroblasts.
  • 🔴 Heparan sulfate binding aids in growth factor localization.
  • 🟢 Fibronectin domains bind TGF-β and bFGF actively.

Topic 2 – Focal Adhesions

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Focal adhesions are integrin-mediated clusters linking ECM to cytoskeleton, transmitting signals and forces.
Mind map: Focal Adhesion → Integrin Binds (Collagen/Fibronectin → RGD) → Cluster Formation → Components (Vinculin/Talin/FAK/Actin/Myosin) → Signals to Nucleus → 2D (Punctate) vs 3D (Diffuse).

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Integrins bind ECM like collagen/fibronectin to form focal adhesions.
  • 🟢 Focal adhesions are cell adhesion clusters.
  • 🔴 Green fluorescence often labels focal adhesions in imaging.
  • 🟢 2D adhesions are punctate; 3D are fibrillar/diffuse.
  • 🔵 Transmit mechanical signals to nucleus via cytoskeleton.
  • 🟢 Involve proteins: vinculin, talin, paxillin, FAK.
  • 🔴 Neural progenitor cells: remodeling > binding in 3D.
  • 🟢 NPC stemness insensitive to adhesive ligands in 3D.
  • 🔵 Used in TE for scaffold design mimicking ECM.
  • 🟢 Forces from actin-myosin contraction.
  • 🔴 Turnover regulates migration.
  • 🟢 FAK phosphorylates for signaling.
  • 🔵 3D environments alter adhesion morphology.

Integrins bind ECM (e.g., fibronectin via RGD), clustering with talin/vinculin/actin to form adhesions transmitting signals.

How do focal adhesions differ in 2D vs 3D?

In 2D, they are punctate for strong anchorage; in 3D, fibrillar for remodeling, as seen in NPC maintenance.

Topic 3 – Cell Migration and Chemotaxis

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Cell migration involves cytoskeleton reorganization for movement, guided by taxis like chemotaxis in development and repair.
Mind map: Migration → Mechanisms (Attachment → Contraction → Detachment) → Cytoskeleton (Actin Polymerization → Myosin Force) → Taxis (Chemo → Gradient; Hapto → Adhesive; Photo → Light) → Applications (Embryogenesis → Wound Healing).

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Cells migrate via attachment, contraction, detachment.
  • 🟢 Not just elastic bags; involve cytoskeleton reorganization.
  • 🔴 Chemotaxis: movement by chemical gradients (e.g., cAMP).
  • 🟢 Polarized morphology in migrating cells.
  • 🔵 Strength depends on concentration and gradient steepness.
  • 🟢 Taxis types: topo (turning to stimulus), klino (less turning oriented), ortho (speed increase oriented).
  • 🔴 Defects cause embryonic malformations, epilepsy.
  • 🟢 In skin repair: cells invade dermal matrix.
  • 🔵 Actin polymerization initiates; myosin maintains.
  • 🟢 Focal adhesions as traction points.
  • 🔴 Neutrophil chases bacterium via chemotaxis.
  • 🟢 Cilia motion for fluid movement.
  • 🔵 Embryonic: blastocyst implantation requires migration.

Explain chemotaxis.

Directed migration responding to chemical gradients, with polarized cells moving toward higher concentrations.

Why is cell migration crucial in embryonic development?

Defects lead to implantation failure, malformed tissues, or congenital abnormalities like epilepsy.

Topic 4 – Signaling Ligands and Types

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Ligands initiate signaling for coordination in tissue development, classified by type, range, and function.
Mind map: Ligands → Molecular (Diffusible → Growth Factors/Steroids; ECM → Proteoglycans/Collagen) → Range (Endocrine → Distant; Paracrine → Nearby; Autocrine → Self; Juxtacrine → Contact) → Function (Growth Factors → VEGF/TGF-β; Hormones/Cytokines/Chemokines).

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Ligands: diffusible (soluble proteins, steroids) or ECM-bound.
  • 🟢 Range: endocrine (distant, blood), paracrine (local), autocrine (self), juxtacrine (contact).
  • 🔴 Growth factors stimulate proliferation/differentiation (VEGF, TGF-β, bFGF, EGF).
  • 🟢 Hormones: messengers via circulation.
  • 🔵 Cytokines affect other cells; chemokines are chemotactic.
  • 🟢 Matrikines: ECM fragments with activity.
  • 🔴 Thrombospondins inhibit angiogenesis.
  • 🟢 Signaling: ligand generation → response (proliferation, migration, ECM production).
  • 🔵 TE applications: growth factors in scaffolds.
  • 🟢 Parathyroid hormone: endocrine for bone remodeling.
  • 🔴 Interleukins: paracrine in skin.
  • 🟢 Autocrine: self-response.
  • 🔵 Juxtacrine requires cell-cell contact.

Name key growth factors in TE.

Topic 5 – Central Paradigm of Signal Transduction

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Signal transduction converts extracellular ligands into intracellular responses via receptors and cascades leading to gene activation.
Mind map: Paradigm → Initiation (Ligand Bind Receptor → Activation) → Transduction (Cascade → TF Activation) → Gene (TF Binds → Transcription → Protein Synthesis).

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Initiation: ligand binds membrane receptor, changes activity.
  • 🟢 Transduction: activates cascade to nuclear TF.
  • 🔴 Gene activation: TF binds regulatory sequences → mRNA/protein.
  • 🟢 TF: DNA-binding proteins; may translocate or activate.
  • 🔵 Involves second messengers (cAMP, Ca2+, NO).
  • 🟢 Transcription: RNA polymerase uses rNTPs.
  • 🔴 Translation: mRNA → protein via ribosomes, start AUG.
  • 🟢 Protein folding: ER → Golgi → secretion.
  • 🔵 mRNA vaccines: modified bases reduce inflammation.
  • 🟢 Protein structure: primary (AA sequence) to quaternary.
  • 🔴 Central dogma: signal → transduction → gene expression.
  • 🟢 Relocalization: e.g., NFκB from cytosol to nucleus.
  • 🔵 Phosphorylation common in cascades.

  • Forgetting second messengers in transduction.
  • Overlooking protein processing post-translation.

  • Paradigm: “I-T-G” – Initiation-Transduction-Gene Activation.
  • Codons: “AUG Starts Translation” – Always Use Gloves (safety hook).

Ligand binds receptor (initiation), triggers cascade (transduction), activates TF for gene expression.

What is gene activation?

TF binds DNA regulatory sequences, increasing transcription rate to produce mRNA and proteins.

Topic 6 – G-Protein Coupled Receptors

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

GPCRs transmit diverse signals via G-protein dissociation, involved in senses and regulation.
Mind map: GPCR → 7 Transmembrane → Ligand Bind → G-Protein (α/β/γ Dissociate) → Effectors (Adenylate Cyclase/PLC) → Second Messengers (cAMP/IP3/Ca2+).

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • 🔴 GPCRs: 7 transmembrane segments.
  • 🟢 Respond to photons, amines, hormones, proteins.
  • 🔴 Agonist binding dissociates G-protein subunits.
  • 🟢 α subunit or β/γ interact with effectors.
  • 🔵 Involved in vision (photoisomerization), smell, immunity.
  • 🟢 Autonomic NS: blood pressure, heart rate.
  • 🔴 Visual: rhodopsin activation.
  • 🟢 Smell: olfactory receptors.
  • 🔵 Immune: chemokine receptors.
  • 🟢 Dissociation: GDP → GTP exchange.

Topic 7 – Receptor Tyrosine Kinases and Pathways

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

RTKs dimerize upon ligand binding, phosphorylating for cascades in growth and angiogenesis.
Mind map: RTK → Ligand (e.g., VEGF) → Dimerization → Phosphorylation → Cascade (Ras/Raf/MEK/ERK) → TF (Ets-1) → Gene Expression.

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • 🔴 RTKs: dimerize, autophosphorylate tyrosines.
  • 🟢 Include VEGF-R, PDGF-R for vessel assembly.
  • 🔴 VEGF: endothelial differentiation.
  • 🟢 PDGF-B: recruits mural cells.
  • 🔵 Cascade: Sos → Ras → Raf → MEK → ERK → Nucleus.
  • 🟢 TGF-β: serine/threonine kinases, Smad pathway.
  • 🔴 BMPs: bone induction via Smads.
  • 🟢 Wnt: canonical (β-catenin → TCF/Lef), noncanonical (polarity/Ca2+).
  • 🔵 Involved in cancer, development.
  • 🟢 LRP5/6 co-receptors in Wnt.
  • 🔴 Fz: GPCR-like in Wnt.

Topic 8 – Mechanotransduction and Cytoskeleton

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Mechanotransduction converts physical forces into biochemical responses via cytoskeleton, vital for bone and vessel health.
Mind map: Forces (Shear/Tension) → Sense (Integrins/Junctions) → Transmit (Cytoskeleton → Actin/MT/IF) → Response (Gene Expression) → Applications (Stress Shielding/Osteoporosis).

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Mechanotransduction: physical stimulus → physiological change.
  • 🟢 Cytoskeleton: actin (microfilaments), microtubules, intermediate filaments.
  • 🔴 Actin: G-actin → F-actin polymerization; (+) end growth.
  • 🟢 Myosin: ATP → force for contraction.
  • 🔵 Treadmilling: add at (+), remove at (-).
  • 🟢 Microtubules: α/β tubulin; dynamic instability.
  • 🔴 MTOC organizes polarity.
  • 🟢 Stress shielding: prosthesis reduces bone load → atrophy.
  • 🔵 Endothelial: shear → alignment via PECAM-1/VE-cad/VEGFR2.
  • 🟢 Fibroblasts contract collagen matrix.
  • 🔴 Wolff’s Law: stress influences bone strength.
  • 🟢 Optogenetics: light-sensitive channels control activity.
  • 🔵 Paclitaxel stabilizes MT, inhibits division.

15-Minute Mock Exam

  1. Explain the role of RGD in cell adhesion and its TE applications.

    RGD (Arg-Gly-Asp) is the integrin-binding site in fibronectin promoting attachment; in TE, short RGD peptides functionalize scaffolds for cell adhesion without full proteins.

  2. Compare focal adhesions in 2D vs 3D environments, with NPC example.

    2D: punctate, ligand-sensitive; 3D: fibrillar, remodeling-focused; for NPCs, 3D stemness maintenance is insensitive to ligands, prioritizing remodeling.

  3. Describe chemotaxis and its measurement.

    Directed migration to chemical gradients; assessed by polarized morphology and proportion of cells moving toward/away from attractant, depending on concentration and gradient steepness.

  4. Outline the central signal transduction paradigm.

    Initiation: ligand-receptor binding; Transduction: cascade activates nuclear TF; Gene activation: TF binds DNA, leading to transcription, translation, and physiological changes.

  5. How does Wnt canonical pathway work?

    Ligand binds Fz/LRP, stabilizes β-catenin, which enters nucleus and interacts with TCF/Lef for gene transcription in development and cancer.
