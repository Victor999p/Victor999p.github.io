Topic 1 – Scaffold Fabrication Principles & Material Selection 支架製備原則與材料選擇

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Scaffold must mimic native ECM 細胞外基質 mechanically and biologically → material choice dictates fabrication method → porosity 孔隙率 & interconnectivity 互連性 are critical for cell migration 細胞遷移/nutrient diffusion 營養擴散.

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • 🔴 Soft tissues 軟組織 (skin 皮膚, tendon 肌腱, liver 肝臟, vessels 血管) → synthetic/natural polymers 合成/天然聚合物
  • 🔴 Hard tissues 硬組織 (bone 骨) → metals 金屬, ceramics 陶瓷, polymer-containing composites 含聚合物複合材料
  • 🔴 Scaffold must have high porosity 高孔隙率 (>90% ideal 理想>90%), large surface area 大表面積, interconnected pores 互連孔隙
  • 🔴 Pore size 孔徑: 100–500 μm generally optimal for most tissues
  • 🟢 Fabrication technique depends on polymer solubility 聚合物溶解性, Tg/Tm, biocompatibility needs 生物相容性需求
  • 🔴 All methods aim to create 3D porous structure 3D多孔結構 for cell infiltration 細胞浸潤 & vascularisation 血管化

Topic 2 – Conventional Porosity Techniques 傳統造孔技術

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Five classic ways to make pores; each has trade-offs in interconnectivity, solvent use, and pore control.

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • 🔴 Solvent-casting + particulate leaching (SCPL) 溶劑鑄造+粒子浸出 – gold standard 金標準, uses salt/sugar, highly interconnected
  • 🔴 Gas foaming 氣體發泡 – no organic solvent 無有機溶劑 (advantage), but mostly closed pores 閉孔
  • 🔴 Gas foaming + particulate leaching 氣體發泡+粒子浸出 – solves interconnectivity problem
  • 🔴 Melt molding 熔融成型 – no solvent, uses heat + porogen 致孔劑 (e.g., gelatin particles)
  • 🔴 Phase separation 相分離 – low temperature, good for incorporating growth factors 生長因子
  • 🔴 Freeze-drying 冷凍乾燥 – emulsion or non-emulsion, pore size controlled by freezing rate/pH

  • 話氣體發泡單獨使用能提供高互連性 (Saying gas foaming alone gives high interconnectivity) - 實際上唔得
  • 忘記聚合物必須不溶於水但致孔劑必須水溶 (Forgetting polymer must be insoluble in water but porogen must be water-soluble)

Topic 3 – Advanced Biofabrication Techniques 先進生物製造技術

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Move from random porosity to precise, reproducible 3D architecture 3D結構 + ability to include cells during printing.

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • 🔴 Electrospinning 靜電紡絲 → nanofibers 納米纖維, excellent for ECM mimicry, cheap & fast
  • 🔴 Rapid prototyping 快速原型 / 3D printing 3D打印 / microstereolithography 微立體光刻 → CAD-CAM control CAD-CAM控制 of exact geometry
  • 🔴 Microsphere sintering 微球燒結 → trabecular bone-like pores 小梁骨樣孔隙
  • 🔴 Decellularized organs 脫細胞器官 → keep native vascular network 血管網絡 (Ott 2008, 2010 – perfused lung 灌注肺)
  • 🟢 Decellularization + recellularization 脫細胞+再細胞化 = whole organ engineering gold standard 全器官工程金標準

Topic 4 – Hydrogels & Cell Encapsulation 水凝膠與細胞封裝

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Hydrogels 水凝膠 = water-swollen polymer networks 水溶脹聚合物網絡; unique ability to encapsulate live cells during gelation.

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • 🔴 Gelation triggers 凝膠化觸發: chemical crosslinking 化學交聯, UV, temperature, ionic (Ca²⁺ for alginate 藻酸鹽鈣交聯)
  • 🔴 Advantages: cells encapsulated in true 3D, cytocompatible gelation possible 細胞兼容性凝膠化
  • 🔴 GelMA, PEG, alginate most common
  • 🔴 Photoencapsulation 光封裝 allows spatial patterning 空間圖案化
  • 🔴 Major limitation inside thick hydrogels: diffusion limit 擴散限制 → necrosis 壞死 in centre after ~200–300 μm

Topic 5 – Limitations of Conventional Scaffolds 傳統支架嘅局限性

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Traditional methods cannot give precise internal channels or full interconnectivity → poor vascularisation in thick constructs.

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts) (memorise the list)

  • Poor control of pore size/geometry/spatial distribution 孔徑/幾何形狀/空間分佈
  • Lack of predefined internal channels 預定義內部通道
  • Incomplete porogen leaching 不完全嘅致孔劑浸出 in thick scaffolds
  • Closed pores 閉孔 in gas foaming (10–30% interconnected)
  • Residual toxic solvents 殘留有毒溶劑
  • Cell seeding inefficient 細胞接種效率低 (cells stay on surface, <500 μm penetration)

Topic 6 – Bioreactors in Tissue Engineering 組織工程中嘅生物反應器

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Static culture fails → bioreactors provide nutrient flow 營養流 & mechanical signals 機械信號.

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • 🔴 Purposes: mass transport 物質傳輸, chemical signals 化學信號, mechanical conditioning 機械調節
  • 🔴 Types: spinner flask 旋轉瓶, rotating wall vessel 旋轉壁容器, perfusion cartridges 灌注盒
  • 🔴 Perfusion 灌注 → uniform cell/matrix distribution throughout scaffold
  • 🟢 Mechanical loading 機械負荷 examples:
    • Cyclic compression 週期性壓縮 → ↑ GAG 糖胺聚糖/collagen in cartilage
    • Stretch 拉伸 → aligned collagen & tenascin 腱生蛋白 in ligament

Topic 7 – Host Response to Implanted Biomaterials 宿主對植入生物材料嘅反應

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Injury → hemostasis 止血 → inflammation 炎症 → granulation 肉芽組織形成 → foreign body reaction 異物反應 → fibrosis/encapsulation 纖維化/包裹.

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • 🔴 Sequence: Hemostasis 止血 → Acute inflammation 急性炎症 (neutrophils 中性粒細胞) → Chronic inflammation 慢性炎症 (macrophages 巨噬細胞) → Granulation tissue 肉芽組織 → Foreign body giant cells 異物巨細胞 → Fibrous capsule 纖維囊
  • 🔴 Biomaterials cause non-specific inflammation 非特異性炎症, NOT immune rejection 非免疫排斥
  • 🔴 Final usual outcome = fibrous encapsulation 纖維包裹 (acellular collagen capsule)
  • 🔴 Capsule thicker with: motion 移動, sharp edges 銳邊, leaching monomers 單體浸出, corrosion 腐蝕

Topic 8 – Immunoisolation & Encapsulated Cell Therapy 免疫隔離與封裝細胞療法

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Physical barrier protects allogeneic/xenogeneic cells 同種異體/異種細胞 from immune attack while allowing nutrient/waste/hormone exchange.

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • 🔴 Classic example: alginate-polylysine microcapsules 藻酸鹽-聚賴氨酸微膠囊 for pancreatic islets 胰島
  • 🔴 Membrane MWCO (Molecular Weight Cut-Off) 分子量截留值 typically 50–150 kDa (lets insulin/glucose through, blocks antibodies)
  • 🔴 Allows use of non-autologous cells 非自體細胞 without immunosuppression 無需免疫抑制

  1. Compare advantages and disadvantages of solvent-casting particulate leaching versus gas foaming/particulate leaching combination. (8 marks)

  2. Explain why conventional scaffold techniques fail to produce clinically relevant thick tissues (>1 cm). Give two biological reasons and one fabrication reason. (6 marks)

    Diffusion limitation 擴散限制 of oxygen/nutrients (<200–300 μm), lack of pre-formed vascular channels 預成型血管通道, poor cell penetration 細胞滲透 during seeding (cells remain on surface 細胞停留在表面).

  4. A company wants to implant insulin-producing cells from pig into diabetic patients without immunosuppression. Suggest and justify a tissue-engineering strategy. (6 marks)

    Encapsulate 封裝 porcine islets 豬胰島 in alginate-polylysine microcapsules 藻酸鹽-聚賴氨酸微膠囊 (immunoisolation 免疫隔離). Membrane permits glucose/insulin diffusion 葡萄糖/胰島素擴散 but excludes immune cells/antibodies 免疫細胞/抗體 → prevents rejection 防止排斥 while allowing function.

  5. State four essential requirements of a tissue engineering bioreactor. (4 marks)

    Controlled temperature/pH 受控溫度/pH, efficient mass transfer 質量傳遞, delivery of chemical signals 化學信號, ability to apply mechanical/electrical signals 機械/電信號.

Topic 5 – Limitations of Conventional Scaffolds

Big Picture

Traditional methods cannot give precise internal channels or full interconnectivity → poor vascularisation in thick constructs.

Must-Know Facts (memorise the list)

  • Poor control of pore size/geometry/spatial distribution
  • Lack of predefined internal channels
  • Incomplete porogen leaching in thick scaffolds
  • Closed pores in gas foaming (10–30% interconnected)
  • Residual toxic solvents
  • Cell seeding inefficient (cells stay on surface, <500 μm penetration)

Topic 6 – Bioreactors in Tissue Engineering

Big Picture

Static culture fails → bioreactors provide nutrient flow & mechanical signals.

Must-Know Facts

  • 🔴 Purposes: mass transport, chemical signals, mechanical conditioning
  • 🔴 Types: spinner flask, rotating wall vessel, perfusion cartridges
  • 🔴 Perfusion → uniform cell/matrix distribution throughout scaffold
  • 🟢 Mechanical loading examples:
    • Cyclic compression → ↑ GAG/collagen in cartilage
    • Stretch → aligned collagen & tenascin in ligament

Topic 7 – Host Response to Implanted Biomaterials

Big Picture

Injury → hemostasis → inflammation → granulation → foreign body reaction → fibrosis/encapsulation.

Must-Know Facts

  • 🔴 Sequence: Hemostasis → Acute inflammation (neutrophils) → Chronic inflammation (macrophages) → Granulation tissue → Foreign body giant cells → Fibrous capsule
  • 🔴 Biomaterials cause non-specific inflammation, NOT immune rejection
  • 🔴 Final usual outcome = fibrous encapsulation (acellular collagen capsule)
  • 🔴 Capsule thicker with: motion, sharp edges, leaching monomers, corrosion

Mnemonics

“Hemostasis → Acute → Chronic → Granulation → Giant cells → Capsule” = HAC3G

Topic 8 – Immunoisolation & Encapsulated Cell Therapy

Big Picture

Physical barrier protects allogeneic/xenogeneic cells from immune attack while allowing nutrient/waste/hormone exchange.

Must-Know Facts

  • 🔴 Classic example: alginate-polylysine microcapsules for pancreatic islets
  • 🔴 Membrane MWCO typically 50–150 kDa (lets insulin/glucose through, blocks antibodies)
  • 🔴 Allows use of non-autologous cells without immunosuppression

15-Minute Mock Exam

  1. Compare advantages and disadvantages of solvent-casting particulate leaching versus gas foaming/particulate leaching combination. (8 marks)

    SCPL: excellent interconnectivity, good pore control, but uses toxic organic solvent. Gas foaming + PL: no solvent (more biocompatible), excellent interconnectivity, retains temperature-sensitive molecules, slightly more complex process.

  2. Explain why conventional scaffold techniques fail to produce clinically relevant thick tissues (>1 cm). Give two biological reasons and one fabrication reason. (6 marks)

    Diffusion limitation of oxygen/nutrients (<200–300 μm), lack of pre-formed vascular channels, poor cell penetration during seeding (cells remain on surface).

  3. Describe the host response timeline from implantation to fibrous encapsulation. (8 marks)

    Injury → hemostasis & platelet activation → acute inflammation (neutrophils) → chronic inflammation (macrophages/monocytes) → granulation tissue + angiogenesis → foreign body giant cells → fibrous capsule formation (weeks–months).

  4. A company wants to implant insulin-producing cells from pig into diabetic patients without immunosuppression. Suggest and justify a tissue-engineering strategy. (6 marks)

    Encapsulate porcine islets in alginate-polylysine microcapsules (immunoisolation). Membrane permits glucose/insulin diffusion but excludes immune cells/antibodies → prevents rejection while allowing function.

  5. State four essential requirements of a tissue engineering bioreactor. (4 marks)

    Controlled temperature/pH, efficient mass transfer, delivery of chemical signals, ability to apply mechanical/electrical signals.
