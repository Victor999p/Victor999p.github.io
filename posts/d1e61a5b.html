Definition of Biomaterials | 生物材料的定義

  • Definition: Biomaterials are materials suitable for making devices to replace a part or function of the body in a safe, reliable, economic, and physiologically acceptable manner. | 生物材料是用於製造安全、可靠、經濟且生理上可接受的裝置，以替代身體部分或功能的材料。
  • Historical Context: The use of biomaterials dates back a long time, but the notion of biomaterials science emerged in the 1960s. | 生物材料的使用歷史悠久，但生物材料科學的概念於1960年代興起。

The definition, though initially restricted, is continually improved and provides an overview of goals, needs, and potential applications. | 該定義雖最初有限，但持續改進，提供了目標、需求和潛在應用的概述。

Clinical Needs for Biomaterials | 生物材料的臨床需求

Stems from inability to treat many diseases, injuries, and conditions: | 源於無法治療許多疾病、傷害和狀況：

  • Replacement of other therapy parts or procedures (total hip) | 替代其他治療部件或程序（全髖關節置換）
  • Correct abnormalities (spinal cord) | 矯正異常（脊髓）
  • Improve function (pacemaker, stent) | 改善功能（心臟起搏器、支架）
  • Structural, pharmaceutical effects: sutures, drug release | 結構與藥物效應：縫合線、藥物釋放

Characteristics of Four Generations | 四個世代的特徵

Generation of Biomaterials

1st-Generation

First Generation | 第一世代:

  • Restricted to materials available in the natura-environment. | 僅限於自然環境中可用的材料。
  • Used to simply repair organs. | 用於簡單修復器官。

2nd-Generation

Second Generation | 第二世代:

  • Spans a long period, starting with improvements in human knowledge and ending in the mid-20th century. | 跨越很長一段時間，從人類知識進步開始，至20世紀中葉結束。
  • Benefited from the Industrial-Revolution. | 受益於工業革命。

3rd-Generation

Third Generation | 第三世代:

  • Began with the birth-of-polymers as promising materials. | 以聚合物的誕生作為有前景的材料開始。
  • Built on scientific advancements in materials and their transformation, as well as understanding of living systems. | 基於材料及其轉化的科學進步，以及對生命系統的理解。

now

Fourth Generation | 第四世代:

  • Currently in its early stages. | 目前處於早期階段。
  • Combines “dreams and realities” with assistance from artificial-intelligence (AI). | 結合“夢想與現實”，得到人工智能（AI）的協助。

Forces in Biomaterials | 生物材料中的作用力

Biomaterials are materials. | 生物材料即材料。

The key to understanding matter is to understand attractive and interactive forces between atoms. | 理解物質的關鍵在於理解原子間的吸引力和交互作用力。

Bioengineering and Chemical Engineering, University of Washington, Seattle, USA

Van der Waals or Dispersion Forces | 范德瓦爾斯或分散力

  • By considering the electron clouds (charge density in space) of two atoms or molecules, both without permanent dipole moments. | 考慮兩個無永久偶極矩的原子或分子的電子雲（空間中的電荷密度）。
  • Electron clouds are contributory in molecular form. | 電子雲在分子形態中起作用。
  • Can shift to one side of the atom/molecule, therefore, at any moment the atoms or molecules can create a fluctuating instantaneous dipole. | 可移到原子/分子的一側，因此，任何時刻原子或分子可產生波動瞬時偶極。
  • The fluctuating instantaneous dipole in one molecule typically induces such an “instantaneous dipole” in the next molecule. | 一個分子的波動瞬時偶極通常在下一個分子中誘導出類似的“瞬時偶極”。
    • They dictate the properties of many materials (for example, some polymers such as polyethylene which has no obvious dipole). | 它們決定了許多材料的性質（例如，無明顯偶極的聚乙烯等聚合物）。
    • They explain why the lipids in cell membranes assemble into the bilayer structure. A typical van der Waals interactive force (for example, CH4 … CH4) is about 9 kJ/mol. | 它們解釋了為什麼細胞膜中的脂質組裝成雙層結構。典型的范德瓦爾斯交互力（例如，CH4 … CH4）約為9 kJ/mol。

Ionic Forces and Hydrogen Bond | 離子力與氫鍵

  • Ionic force | 離子力:
    • The + and - charges are arrayed to achieve the closest interaction of opposite charges, and the furthest separation of similar charges. | 正負電荷排列以實現相反電荷的最密切交互，並使相同電荷分離最遠。
    • Typical ionic bond strength: (for example, NaCl) are about 770 kJ/mol. | 典型的離子鍵強度：（例如，氯化鈉）約為770 kJ/mol。
  • Hydrogen bond | 氫鍵:
    • Typical hydrogen bond strengths (for example, O-H … H) are about 20 kJ/mol. | 典型的氫鍵強度（例如，O-H … H）約為20 kJ/mol。

Metallic and Covalent Bonds | 金屬鍵與共價鍵

  • Single metallic bond is rarely discussed. The total interactive strength is realized through the multiplicity of the plus-minus interactions, at 25°C, aluminum will have a heat of sublimation of 325 kJ/mol, while titanium will be about 475 kJ/mol. | 單個金屬鍵很少被討論。總交互強度通過多重正負交互實現，在25°C時，鋁的升華熱約為325 kJ/mol，而鈦約為475 kJ/mol。
  • Covalent bonds are relatively strong bonds associated with sharing of pairs of electrons between atoms. Typical covalent bond strengths (for example, C-C) are about 350 kJ/mol. | 共價鍵是原子間共享電子對的相對強鍵。典型的共價鍵強度（例如，C-C）約為350 kJ/mol。

Ionically-bonded Hydrogels/Natural Polymer | 離子鍵結水凝膠/天然聚合物

For example | 例如:

  • Base acid: guluronic acid, mannuroic acid, Alginate (polysaccharide) | 基礎酸：古洛糖酸、甘露糖酸、海藻酸（多醣）
    By adding | 通過添加:
    • Cationic polymer (e.g. chitosan fraction, polylysine) | 陽離子聚合物（例如，幾丁聚醣片段、聚賴氨酸）
    • Divalent cations: (Creating salt bridge e.g. Ca2+) | 二價陽離子：（創建鹽橋，例如Ca2+）
      Form GELS using Ions or Polymers | 使用離子或聚合物形成凝膠

Collagen, the Most Abundant Protein in Mammals | 膠原蛋白，哺乳動物中最豐富的蛋白質

  • ~30% of total protein mass | 約佔總蛋白質質量的30%
  • Deposited in the extracellular matrix | 沉積於細胞外基質
  • Since the discovery of collagen II by Miller and Matukas (1969), 26 new collagen types have been found | 自米勒和馬圖卡斯（1969）發現膠原蛋白II以來，已發現26種新膠原蛋白類型
  • The common structural feature of collagens is the presence of a triple helix that can range from most of their structure (96% for collagen I) to less than 10% (collagen XII) | 膠原蛋白的共同結構特徵是存在三螺旋結構，範圍從大部分結構（膠原蛋白I為96%）到不到10%（膠原蛋白XII）
  • Collagen α chains vary in size from 662 up to 3152 amino acids | 膠原蛋白α鏈的大小從662到3152個氨基酸不等
  • The three α chains can be either identical to form homotrimers (e.g., collagen II) or different to form heterotrimers (e.g., collagen IX) | 三條α鏈可以相同形成同三聚體（例如，膠原蛋白II）或不同形成異三聚體（例如，膠原蛋白IX）

By Cold Spring Harb Perspect Biol. 2011 Jan; 3(1): a004978

Molecular Assemblies: Collagen Hierarchical Structures | 分子組裝：膠原蛋白層次結構

  • MOLECULAR ASSEMBLIES | 分子組裝:
    • α-chains -> collagen molecules (triple helices) -> collagen fibrils -> collagen fibers | α鏈 -> 膠原蛋白分子（三螺旋） -> 膠原蛋白纖維 -> 膠原蛋白纖維束
  • Collagen fibers scale | 膠原蛋白纖維尺度:
    • Up to 10s of microns, 10-500 nm | 高達數十微米，10-500奈米
  • Amino acids in the alpha chain are proline (PRO), glycine (GLY), and hydroxyproline (HYP). | α鏈中的氨基酸為脯氨酸（PRO）、甘氨酸（GLY）和羥脯氨酸（HYP）。

Types of Collagen | 膠原蛋白類型

  • TYPE 1: Skin, Nails, and Hair | 皮膚、指甲和頭髮
    • Found in ~90% of the collagen in the body. Forms 80% of dermis of skin and is the most beneficial for skin, nails, and hair. | 佔體內約90%的膠原蛋白，形成皮膚真皮的80%，對皮膚、指甲和頭髮最有益。
  • TYPE 2: Elastic Cartilage, e.g., Ear | 彈性軟骨，例如耳朵
    • Found in elastic cartilage like the ear. This type is linked to anti-ageing properties. | 存在於耳朵等彈性軟骨中，與抗衰老特性相關。
  • TYPE 3: Intestines, Blood Vessels, Muscles | 腸道、血管、肌肉
    • Occurs in the intestines and has been linked to blood vessels and muscles. | 存在於腸道，與血管和肌肉相關。
  • TYPE 4: Base Membranes for Organs | 器官基底膜
    • Found in egg whites, this collagen forms the base also forms the base membranes for organs, including the kidney. | 存在於蛋清中，形成器官基底膜，包括腎臟。
  • TYPE 5: Cornea & Embryonic Development | 角膜與胚胎發育
    • Found in the cornea. Type 5 also helps with embryonic development. | 存在於角膜。第五類型還有助於胚胎發育。

In-stent Restenosis | 支架內再狹窄

Most Successful Invention | 最成功的發明

  • Stent story | 支架故事
  • Video: https://youtu.be/f92O0iT2Odo
  • Stent is a metal or plastic tube inserted into the lumen of an anatomic vessel or duct to keep the passageway open, and stenting is the placement of a stent. | 支架是插入解剖血管或管道內腔的金屬或塑料管，以保持通道暢通，支架植入術是支架的放置。
  • The first use of a coronary stent is typically attributed to Jacques Puel and Ulrich Sigwart when they implanted a stent into a patient in Toulouse, France, in 1986. | 冠狀動脈支架的首次使用通常歸功於雅克·普埃爾和烏爾里希·西格瓦特，他們於1986年在法國圖盧茲為患者植入支架。

Pathophysiology of Restenosis | 再狹窄的病理生理學

  • The initial consequences immediately after stent placement are de-endothelialization, crush of the plaque, and stretch of the entire artery. | 支架放置後的即時後果包括去內皮化、斑塊壓碎和整個動脈的拉伸。
  • A layer of platelets and fibrin is then deposited at the injured site. Activated platelets on the surface expressing adhesion molecules such as P-selectin attach circulating leukocytes via platelet receptors such as P-selectin glycoprotein (GP) ligand. The leukocytes begin a process of rolling along the injured surface and migrate across the platelet-fibrin layer and into the tissue driven by chemical gradients of chemokines released from SMCs and resident macrophages. | 隨後在受傷部位沉積一層血小板和纖維蛋白。表面活化的血小板表達P-選擇素等黏附分子，通過血小板受體（如P-選擇素糖蛋白配體）附著循環白細胞。白細胞開始沿受傷表面滾動，並在平滑肌細胞（SMC）和駐留巨噬細胞釋放的趨化因子的化學梯度驅動下，穿越血小板-纖維蛋白層並遷移到組織中。
  • Next is a granulation or cellular proliferation phase. Growth factors are subsequently released from platelets and leukocytes, which stimulate migration of smooth muscle cells (SMCs) from the media into the neointima. The resultant neointima consists of SMCs, extracellular matrix, and macrophages recruited over several weeks. | 接下來是肉芽組織或細胞增殖階段。血小板和白細胞隨後釋放生長因子，刺激平滑肌細胞（SMC）從中膜遷移到新生內膜。最終新生內膜由平滑肌細胞、細胞外基質和數週內招募的巨噬細胞組成。
  • Over longer time periods, the artery enters a phase of remodeling involving extracellular matrix (ECM) protein degradation and resynthesis. Accompanying this phase is a shift to fewer cellular elements and greater production of ECM. | 在更長的時間內，動脈進入涉及細胞外基質（ECM）蛋白降解和再合成的重塑階段。此階段伴隨著細胞成分減少和ECM生成增加。

By Circulation. 2005; 111:237-273

Minimally Invasive Surgery | 微創手術

  • Definition: Uses small incisions instead of large ones, enabling complex procedures with: | 定義：使用小切口而非大切口，使複雜手術具備：
