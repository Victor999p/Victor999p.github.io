Topic 1 – Tissue Engineering Paradigm 組織工程範式

大圖景 (Big Picture)

The tissue engineering paradigm integrates cells 細胞, biocompatible materials 生物相容性材料, biochemical factors 生化因子 like growth factors 生長因子, and biophysical factors 生物物理因子 such as cyclic mechanical loading 週期性機械負荷 to regenerate tissues.

思維導圖 (Mind map):
Paradigm 範式 → Cells 細胞 (source 來源) → Materials 材料 (scaffolds 支架) → Biochemical 生化因子 (growth factors 生長因子) → Biophysical 生物物理因子 (mechanical loading 機械負荷) → Outcomes 結果 (tissue regeneration 組織再生)

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • Core components 核心組成部分: cells 細胞, biocompatible materials 生物相容性材料, biochemical factors 生化因子 (e.g., growth factors), biophysical factors 生物物理因子 (e.g., cyclic mechanical loading).
  • Aims to regenerate 3D tissues using a multidisciplinary approach 多學科方法.
  • Cells 細胞 provide the building blocks for new tissue formation.
  • Materials 材料 act as scaffolds 支架 for cell attachment 細胞黏附 and growth 生長.
  • Biochemical factors 生化因子 stimulate cell proliferation 細胞增殖 and differentiation 分化.
  • Biophysical factors 生物物理因子 mimic in vivo 體內 mechanical environments to guide tissue development.
  • Paradigm emphasizes biomimicry 仿生 of natural ECM 細胞外基質.
  • Applications include hard tissues 硬組織 like bone and soft tissues 軟組織 like vascular.
  • Challenges: controlling differentiation, proliferation, and immune response 免疫反應.
  • Mechanical environment 機械環境 plays a key role in cell signaling 細胞信號傳導 and phenotype 表型.
  • Scaffolds must support nutrient diffusion 營養擴散 and vascularization 血管化.
  • Cyclic stretching 週期性拉伸 is essential for constructs like myocardium 心肌.
  • Integration of factors ensures synchronized tissue function 同步的組織功能.

  • 混淆生物物理因子同生化因子 (Mistaking biophysical for biochemical): 考官可能會要求區分兩者效果；唔好混淆機械負荷同生長因子。
  • 忽略整合性 (Overlooking integration): 陷阱題目會問範式而不提及所有四個組成部分。
  • 假設所有因子作用相同 (Assuming all factors are equal): 答案中經常會忽視生物物理因子嘅重要性。

Topic 2 – Tensegrity Model 張拉整體模型

大圖景 (Big Picture)

The tensegrity model 張拉整體模型 describes cells as prestressed structures 預應力結構 where tension 張力 and compression 壓力 elements balance forces, enabling rapid mechanical signal transmission for mechanotransduction 機械轉導.

思維導圖 (Mind map):
Tensegrity 張拉整體 → Architectural 結構性 (tension-compression synergy 張力-壓力協同) → Cellular 細胞性 (actin tension 肌動蛋白張力, microtubules compression 微管壓力) → Pre-stress 預應力 (contractile tension 收縮張力) → Force Propagation 力傳播 (rapid, long-distance 快速，長距離)

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • Championed by Don Ingber as alternative to elastic bag model 彈性袋模型.
  • Cells and nuclei are “hard-wired 硬連線“ to respond to physical stimuli via integrins 整合素.
  • Tension elements 張力元素: actin filaments 肌動蛋白絲; compression 壓力元素: microtubules 微管.
  • Pre-stress 預應力 from actomyosin contraction 肌動球蛋白收縮 balances forces.
  • Stabilizes cell structure and morphology 形態.
  • Forces transmitted at ECM/cell contacts ECM/細胞接觸點, not equally over surface.
  • Inhibited by integrin antagonists 整合素拮抗劑 (shear stress, strain effects).
  • Provides basis for mechanotransduction 機械轉導 linking physical to molecular events.
  • Explains transmission to nucleus.
  • Synergy between balanced tension and compression.
  • Architectural system 結構系統 stabilizes by counteracting forces.
  • Applies to natural and artificial forms (Buckminster Fuller).
  • Prestressed structures 預應力結構 promote long-distance force propagation 長距離力傳播.
  • Faster than chemical signals (1000x via cytoskeleton).
  • Contrasts with homogeneous material models 均質材料模型 where forces decay rapidly.

  • 忘記預應力 (Forgetting pre-stress): 陷阱題在解釋力平衡時不提肌動球蛋白。
  • 混淆元素 (Confusing elements): 混淆張力（肌動蛋白）和壓力（微管）。
  • 低估速度 (Underestimating speed): 考官測試點解機械信號傳遞快過化學信號。

Topic 3 – Mechanoreceptors 機械感受器

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Mechanoreceptors 機械感受器 are plasma membrane proteins 質膜蛋白 that sense external mechanical signals and relay them intracellularly, including integrins 整合素, cadherins 鈣黏蛋白, and stretch-activated ion channels 牽張激活離子通道.

思維導圖 (Mind map):
Mechanoreceptors 機械感受器 → Integrins 整合素 (ECM-cytoskeleton link ECM-細胞骨架連結) → Cadherins 鈣黏蛋白 (cell-cell adhesion 細胞間黏附) → Ion Channels 離子通道 (ion flux on stretch 拉伸時離子流) → Outcomes 結果 (signaling 信號傳導, gene expression 基因表達)

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • Located in plasma membrane 質膜 to sense and relay signals.
  • Integrins 整合素: Link ECM to cytoskeleton via focal adhesions 黏著斑, alter cytoskeleton directly.
  • Cadherins 鈣黏蛋白: Form adherens junctions 黏附連接 for cell-cell force transmission.
  • Stretch-activated ion channels 牽張激活離子通道: Selective for Na+, K+, Ca2+, Cl-; involved in signaling and gene expression.
  • Activated by membrane deformation 膜變形; may activate or inactivate on stretch.
  • Produce intracellular consequences like Ca2+ flux 鈣離子流.
  • Regulate actin polymerization 肌動蛋白聚合 and contact stability 接觸穩定性.
  • Involved in cell signaling, transcription 轉錄, translation 翻譯, protein synthesis 蛋白質合成.
  • Electrochemical gradient 電化學梯度 drives ion movement.
  • Conformational changes 構象變化 with deformation.
  • High ion selectivity 離子選擇性.
  • Essential for mechanotransduction 機械轉導.
  • Cadherins may regulate proliferative signaling 增殖信號 via β-catenin β-連環蛋白.
  • Integrins transmit shear stress 剪切應力 signals.

  • 忽略失活 (Overlooking deactivation): 部分通道拉伸時會失活。

Topic 4 – Integrins 整合素 and Focal Adhesions 黏著斑

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Integrins 整合素 are transmembrane proteins 跨膜蛋白 mediating cell-ECM binding 細胞-ECM結合 and force transmission, forming focal adhesions 黏著斑 that mature under tension 張力 to activate signaling pathways 信號通路.

思維導圖 (Mind map):
Integrins 整合素 → Ligand Binding 配體結合 → Focal Complexes 黏著複合體 (precursor 前體) → Focal Adhesions 黏著斑 (mature 成熟) → Signaling 信號 (FAK, ERK) → Cellular Responses 細胞響應 (shape 形狀, motility 運動性)

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • Integrins elicit signaling similar to shear stress in endothelial cells 內皮細胞.
  • Transmit forces from ECM to cytoskeleton 細胞骨架.
  • Convert physical stimuli to bioelectrical/biochemical signals 生物電/生化信號.
  • Focal complexes 黏著複合體: Small, punctate 點狀, at cell periphery 細胞邊緣; precursors to FAs.
  • Move centripetally 向心地, elongate into FAs.
  • Focal adhesions 黏著斑: Larger, proximal 近端; require tension 張力 for development.
  • Formation: Integrin occupation 整合素佔據, clustering 聚集, tensile force 張力 exertion.
  • Involve proteins like vinculin, talin, paxillin.
  • Shear stress 剪切應力 recruits FAK to leading edge 前沿.
  • Tyrosine phosphorylation 酪氨酸磷酸化 leads to ERK activation.
  • FAK mutant FAK突變體 blocks ERK response to stress.
  • Integrin-ECM dynamics 整合素-ECM動態 altered by shear.
  • Forces travel to FAs via ECM or cytoskeleton.
  • Essential for mechanobiology 機械生物學 responses.

  • 混淆FC同FA (Mixing FC and FA): 陷阱題關於時間線或位置。
  • 忘記張力作用 (Forgetting tension role): 答案缺少”張力”對於成熟嘅描述。
  • 忽略信號傳導 (Ignoring signaling): 考官測試FAK-ERK聯繫。

Topic 5 – Mechanosignaling Pathways 機械信號通路

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Mechanosignaling pathways 機械信號通路 convert mechanical stimuli at cell surfaces into intracellular responses like gene expression 基因表達, involving integrins 整合素, VEGF, Wnt, and nuclear transmission 核傳遞.

思維導圖 (Mind map):
Stimuli 刺激 (Shear/Stretch 剪切/拉伸) → Receptors 受體 (Integrins/Cadherins 整合素/鈣黏蛋白) → Pathways 通路 (FAK/ERK, VEGF, Wnt) → Nucleus 細胞核 (SUN proteins, chromatin 染色質) → Responses 響應 (Differentiation/Proliferation 分化/增殖)

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • Mechanobiology 機械生物學: Responses to shear/stretch at focal adhesions.
  • Pathways: FAK activation, ERK via tyrosine phosphorylation 酪氨酸磷酸化.
  • Shear causes FAK recruitment and rear disappearance.
  • VEGF: Activates Ras-Raf-MEK-ERK pathway, phosphorylates Ets-1 for angiogenic genes 血管生成基因.
  • Nuclear transmission 核傳遞: SUN proteins link to lamina 核纖層, chromatin 染色質 via MARs (Matrix Attachment Regions) 基質附着區.
  • Nuclear actin/myosin 核肌動蛋白/肌球蛋白 control gene positioning 基因定位 and activation.
  • Adherens junctions 黏附連接: Similar to FAs, dynamic link to actin.
  • Tension 張力 at junctions stabilizes AJs, regulates β-catenin signaling β-連環蛋白信號.
  • Wnt pathway: β-catenin dissociation to nucleus for gene expression.
  • Cadherins require force for separation.
  • Vinculin localizes to AJs, binds cytoskeletal proteins.
  • Mechanical signals to nucleus within milliseconds 毫秒.
  • Involved in proliferation, apoptosis 凋亡, differentiation.
  • Examples: Ca2+ flux 鈣離子流 at junctions strengthens contacts.

  • 通路混淆 (Pathway mix-up): 混淆VEGF和Wnt；考官測試序列。
  • 忘記核連結 (Forgetting nuclear link): 陷阱題在解釋毫秒級響應時忽略。
  • 忽略β-連環蛋白 (Overlooking β-catenin): 答案缺少核轉位。

Topic 6 – Effects of Mechanical Forces on Cells 機械力對細胞嘅影響

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Mechanical forces 機械力 influence cell shape 細胞形狀, viability 活力, motility 運動性, differentiation 分化, and proliferation 增殖, switching genetic programs and guiding tissue formation.

思維導圖 (Mind map):
Forces 力 → Cell Shape 細胞形狀 (spreading/deformation 鋪展/變形) → Viability 活力 (apoptosis/growth 凋亡/生長) → Motility 運動性 (lamellipodia 板狀偽足) → Differentiation 分化 (osteo/myogenic 成骨/成肌) → Proliferation 增殖 (stress accumulation 應力積累)

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • Cell shape changes from force balance 力平衡; spreading if ECM stiffer than cytoskeleton.
  • Nucleus deforms, switches apoptosis/differentiation programs 凋亡/分化程序.
  • Geometric control 幾何控制: Micropatterns 微圖案 determine apoptosis vs growth via focal adhesions.
  • Motility 運動性: Lamellipodia 板狀偽足 at stress concentrations.
  • Differentiation 分化: Acto-myosin contractility 肌動球蛋白收縮性 promotes osteogenesis 成骨.
  • Myotube 肌管: Striations 橫紋 on muscle-stiffness gels 肌肉硬度凝膠.
  • Stiffness 硬度 directs lineage 譜系: Soft for neuron 神經元, medium muscle 肌肉, hard bone 骨.
  • Cadherins: Force induces Ca2+ flux 鈣離子流, actin polymerization 肌動蛋白聚合.
  • Traction microscopy 牽引顯微鏡: Measures force during differentiation.
  • Proliferation 增殖: High at edges/sharp geometries; Rho inhibition Rho抑制 abolishes.
  • Myocardium construct 心肌構建體: Neonatal cells + collagen, cyclic stretch 週期性拉伸 for synchronization.
  • Morphologically similar to adult tissue after 6 days.
  • Tissue shape determines stress accumulation 應力積累 for growth.
  • Mechanical precedent 機械先導 in differentiation.

  • 泛化增殖 (Generalizing proliferation): 增殖在邊緣高，並非均勻。
  • 忘記先導性 (Forgetting precedence): 機械變化先於分化發生。

Topic 7 – Scaffolds Introduction and Requirements 支架簡介與要求

大圖景 (Big Picture)

Scaffolds 支架 are artificial ECMs 人工細胞外基質 providing 3D support for cell growth, nutrient diffusion 營養擴散, and tissue regeneration, with requirements for porosity 孔隙率, biocompatibility 生物相容性, and mechanical matching 機械匹配.

思維導圖 (Mind map):
Scaffolds 支架 → Functions 功能 (support 支撐, degrade 降解, diffuse 擴散) → Requirements 要求 (3D porous 3D多孔, biocompatible 生物相容性, surface chemistry 表面化學, mechanical 機械性) → Biomimetic 仿生 (mimic ECM 模仿ECM) → Applications 應用 (bone 骨, vascular 血管)

必須掌握嘅要點 (Must-Know Facts)

  • Temporary/permanent artificial ECM 人工ECM for 3D regeneration.
  • ECM 細胞外基質: Macromolecules (proteins/carbs 蛋白質/碳水化合物) as space fillers 空間填充物.
  • 2D/3D structures; promote cell growth, degrade.
  • Allow nutrient/waste diffusion 營養/廢物擴散, cell migration 細胞遷移.
  • Encourage blood vessel formation 血管形成.
  • Biomimetic 仿生: Apply natural methods to engineering.
  • Examples: Trabecular 小梁結構 for bone, triphasic 三相 for bone-ligament.
  • Requirements: 3D porous interconnected network 多孔互連網絡.
  • Biocompatible 生物相容性, bioresorbable 生物可吸收 with controllable degradation.
  • Suitable surface for attachment/proliferation/differentiation 黏附/增殖/分化.
  • Mechanical properties 機械特性 match implantation site 植入部位.
  • High porosity 孔隙率 for cell colonization 細胞定植.
  • Tunable biodegradability 生物降解性.
  • Biocompatibility 生物相容性 prevents immune reaction 免疫反應.

  • Natural examples: Collagen 膠原蛋白, agarose 瓊脂糖, heparin 肝素.
  • Synthetic: Metals, ceramics, polymers.

  • Synthetic enable specific properties and shapes.
  • Bioinert: Fibrous capsule formation 纖維囊形成.

  • Mg alloys 鎂合金: Bioresorbable 生物可吸收, osteoconductive 骨傳導性, but rapid dissolution risk (hypermagnesia 高鎂血症).

Topic 2 – Tensegrity Model

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

The tensegrity model describes cells as prestressed structures where tension and compression elements balance forces, enabling rapid mechanical signal transmission for mechanotransduction.

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • Championed by Don Ingber as alternative to elastic bag model.
  • Cells and nuclei are “hard-wired” to respond to physical stimuli via integrins.
  • Tension elements: actin filaments; compression: microtubules.
  • Pre-stress from actomyosin contraction balances forces.
  • Stabilizes cell structure and morphology.
  • Forces transmitted at ECM/cell contacts, not equally over surface.
  • Inhibited by integrin antagonists (shear stress, strain effects).
  • Provides basis for mechanotransduction linking physical to molecular events.
  • Explains transmission to nucleus.
  • Synergy between balanced tension and compression.
  • Architectural system stabilizes by counteracting forces.
  • Applies to natural and artificial forms (Buckminster Fuller).
  • Prestressed structures promote long-distance force propagation.
  • Faster than chemical signals (1000x via cytoskeleton).
  • Contrasts with homogeneous material models where forces decay rapidly.

Mechanical forces influence cell shape, viability, motility, differentiation, and proliferation, switching genetic programs and guiding tissue formation.

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • Cell shape changes from force balance; spreading if ECM stiffer than cytoskeleton.
  • Nucleus deforms, switches apoptosis/differentiation programs.
  • Geometric control: Micropatterns determine apoptosis vs growth via focal adhesions.
  • Motility: Lamellipodia at stress concentrations.
  • Differentiation: Acto-myosin contractility promotes osteogenesis.
  • Myotube: Striations on muscle-stiffness gels.
  • Stiffness directs lineage: Soft for neuron, medium muscle, hard bone.
  • Cadherins: Force induces Ca2+ flux, actin polymerization.
  • Traction microscopy: Measures force during differentiation.
  • Proliferation: High at edges/sharp geometries; Rho inhibition abolishes.
  • Myocardium construct: Neonatal cells + collagen, cyclic stretch for synchronization.
  • Morphologically similar to adult tissue after 6 days.
  • Tissue shape determines stress accumulation for growth.
  • Mechanical precedent in differentiation.

Topic 7 – Scaffolds Introduction and Requirements

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Scaffolds are artificial ECMs providing 3D support for cell growth, nutrient diffusion, and tissue regeneration, with requirements for porosity, biocompatibility, and mechanical matching.

  • Temporary/permanent artificial ECM for 3D regeneration.
  • ECM: Macromolecules (proteins/carbs) as space fillers.
  • 2D/3D structures; promote cell growth, degrade.
  • Allow nutrient/waste diffusion, cell migration.
  • Encourage blood vessel formation.
  • Biomimetic: Apply natural methods to engineering.
  • Examples: Trabecular for bone, triphasic for bone-ligament.
  • Requirements: 3D porous interconnected network.
  • Biocompatible, bioresorbable with controllable degradation.
  • Suitable surface for attachment/proliferation/differentiation.
  • Mechanical properties match implantation site.
  • High porosity for cell colonization.
  • Tunable biodegradability.
  • Biocompatibility prevents immune reaction.

  • Mechanical strength for load-bearing (cartilage/bone).

  • No toxic products released.

Topic 9 – Types of Scaffolds

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Scaffolds are classified by biocompatibility as bioinert (minimal interaction), bioactive (interacts with tissue), or bioresorbable (dissolves and replaced by tissue), and by material as natural or synthetic.

Mind map: Types → Biocompatibility (Inert/Active/Resorbable) → Material (Natural/Synthetic) → Examples (Ti inert, HA active, TCP resorbable)

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • Bioinert: Minimal tissue interaction (e.g., Titanium).
  • Bioactive: Bonds to bone/soft tissue (e.g., HA).
  • Bioresorbable: Dissolves, replaced by tissue (e.g., Tricalcium phosphate).
  • Classification of bioceramics: Inert (dental implants), bioactive (HA coating), surface active (bioglass), resorbable (TCP).
  • Natural: Biologically active, complex structure, high cost, pathogenic risks.
  • Synthetic: Modifiable, cheap, reproducible, but degradation/biocompatibility concerns.
  • Natural examples: Collagen, agarose, heparin.
  • Synthetic: Metals, ceramics, polymers.
  • Natural suffice most criteria but limited modification.
  • Synthetic enable specific properties and shapes.
  • Bioinert: Fibrous capsule formation.
  • Bioactive: Bonding within 30 days.
  • Resorbable: Disappears 10-30 days.
  • Composites combine fillers in matrix.

Natural offer biological activity and complex structures but at higher cost and risk; synthetic are modifiable and reproducible but may have degradation issues.

Topic 10 – Scaffold Materials (Metals and Ceramics)

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Scaffold materials include metals (e.g., Ti, Mg) for strength and ceramics (e.g., HA, bioglass) for bioactivity, each with advantages in biocompatibility but limitations in toxicity or mechanics.

Mind map: Materials → Metals (Ti scaffolds, Mg alloys) → Ceramics (CaP, HA, bioglass) → Properties (corrosion resistance, resorbable) → Applications (orthopedic, dental)

Must-Know Facts (max 15 bullet points)

  • Metals: High strength, but lack biological recognition; surface modification needed.
  • Possible toxic ion release from corrosion/wear.
  • Examples: 316L SS for stents, Ti alloys for bone replacement.
  • Ti: High strength, light, corrosion resistant, biocompatible; porous for fixation.
  • Bioinert, slow bone bonding; BMP-loading enhances.
  • Mg alloys: Bioresorbable, osteoconductive, but rapid dissolution risk (hypermagnesia).
  • Ceramics: Refractory, inert, biocompatible; hard, brittle.
  • CaP: Bioactive, resorbable; poor mechanics, used as coatings.
  • HA: Similar to bone mineral, supports ingrowth; for augmentation.
  • Bioglass: Bonds to tissues, composition SiO2-CaO-Na2O; bone cement.
  • Bioactivity regions: A (bonding), B (inert), C (resorbable), D (non-glass).
  • Resorbable: Replaced by bone without mechanical loss.
  • Metals for hard tissues, polymers for soft.
  • Challenges: Toxicity, corrosion for metals; brittleness for ceramics.

15-Minute Mock Exam

  1. Explain the tensegrity model and its role in mechanotransduction.

    The tensegrity model views cells as prestressed structures with actin (tension) and microtubules (compression), enabling rapid force transmission from surface to nucleus, linking physical stimuli to molecular events.

  2. Describe focal adhesions and their formation.

    Focal adhesions are mature integrin-based structures requiring tension; formed by ligand occupation, clustering, and force exertion, involving proteins like FAK and vinculin for signaling.

  3. How do mechanical forces influence cell differentiation?

    Forces alter shape and contractility; e.g., high contractility promotes osteogenesis, while stiffness directs lineages (soft: neuron, medium: muscle, hard: bone) via mechanotransduction.

  4. What are the requirements for an ideal scaffold?

    3D porous interconnected network, biocompatible/bioresorbable with matched degradation, suitable surface chemistry, and mechanical properties aligning with implantation site.

  5. Compare natural and synthetic scaffold materials.

    Natural: Biologically active but costly and risky; synthetic: Modifiable, cheap, but potential toxicity from degradation.
