Topic 1 – Morphogenesis 形態發生

Big Picture

形態發生就係由受精卵變成有形有體嘅過程，係大自然百萬年演化出嚟嘅最佳「修補藍圖」，組織工程學要抄佢功課先至做到真正修復。

Must-Know Facts（記晒呢12粒）

  • 字源：Morphology（形態）+ Generation（生成）🔴
  • 定義：Pattern formation + Body plan + 左右對稱（mirror-image bilateral symmetry）→ 成人形態🔴
  • 點解同組織工程有關？因為自然修補機制係經過百萬年優化，抄佢最穩陣🔵
  • 要考慮嘅四樣嘢（OIGM）：
    1. Origin 細胞來源
    2. Interaction 細胞同ECM/鄰居互動
    3. Growth factors 生長因子
    4. Mechanisms 點樣令未分化細胞變專門細胞🔴

Mnemonics（廣東話記憶法）

O-I-G-M → 「愛（O）姨（I）姑（G）媽（M）」＝Origin-Interaction-Growth factors-Mechanisms

Topic 2 – Embryonic Development Stages 胚胎早期發育

Big Picture

受精卵 → 卵裂 → 桑椹胚 → 囊胚 → 著床 → 原腸形成（三胚層出現）

Must-Know Facts

  • Zygote 受精卵 → Cleavage 卵裂（唔長大，只係分多粒細細胞，仍然全能 totipotent）🔴
  • Morula 桑椹胚（8-cell開始壓實 compaction）
  • Blastocyst 囊胚：外層 Trophectoderm（變胎盤），內層 Inner cell mass（變嬰兒）
  • Gastrulation 原腸形成：出現 Primitive streak 原條 + Node（最前嘅 organizer）
  • Human 特有：平面盤狀（planar）+ 羊膜腔 + 卵黃囊

順序記憶（廣東話）

「精（Zygote）→裂（Cleavage）→桑（Morula）→囊（Blastocyst）→條（Primitive streak）→三層」

Topic 3 – Germ Layers 三胚層來源表（死記表）

胚層主要組織（廣東話記憶）
Ectoderm 外胚層皮膚、頭髮、神經系統、眼角膜、牙釉質
Mesoderm 中胚層所有肌肉、骨、軟骨、血管、心臟、腎、皮膚真皮層
Endoderm 內胚層消化道、肺襯裏、肝、胰、甲狀腺、膀胱

記憶法：外中內 → 「外神經、中肌肉、內腸肺」

Topic 4 – Epithelial-Mesenchymal Transition (EMT) 上皮-間質轉化

Big Picture

上皮細胞（成張貼實）↔ 間質細胞（四處走）嘅轉變，係所有組織塑形嘅核心機制

特點Epithelial 上皮Mesenchymal 間質
形態鋪石樣（cobblestone）紡錘形、會爬
極性有頂-底極性無極性
黏附緊黏（E-cadherin）鬆散（N-cadherin）
行為保護、屏障遷移、入侵

5大變化（考試最愛問）：

  1. 細胞分裂方向改變
  2. 細胞形狀變
  3. 細胞遷移
  4. 生長/死亡
  5. 分泌改變

Topic 5 – Blood Vessel Development 血管發育

Big Picture

第三週因為擴散唔夠用，要自己起血管網

方式Vasculogenesis 原位血管發生Angiogenesis 血管新生
發生位置無血管區直接起由已有血管長枝
代表時間早期胚胎之後生長/傷口癒合
考試陷阱初生血管網＝Vasculogenesis之後分支＝Angiogenesis

Lumen 形成兩大模型：

  1. Cell hollowing（細胞自己挖空）
  2. Cord hollowing（細胞排隊再拆黏附）← 近年證據多數支持呢個

Topic 6 – Branching Morphogenesis 分支形態發生（肺/腎常見）

Big Picture

FGF10 誘導 + Shh/BMP4 抑制 → 形成分支
最新解釋：Turing 反應-擴散模型（activator BMP-2 + inhibitor MGP）

三種肺分支模式：

  1. Domain branching（低抑制物消耗）
  2. Orthogonal branching（高抑制物消耗）
  3. Planar branching（邊界效應）

Topic 7 – Left-Right Asymmetry 左右不對稱

Big Picture

點解心臟喺左邊？唔係純粹隨機！

兩大機制：

  1. Node 纖毛左掃流 → 形態素梯度
  2. 單細胞手性（chirality）← 連雙胞胎都可能相反，證明好早期已有

Topic 1 – Morphogenesis

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Morphogenesis is the developmental process shaping pattern formation, body plan, and symmetry in organisms, crucial for tissue engineering to mimic natural repair.
Mind map: Morphogenesis → Definition (Morphology + Generation) → Cascade (Pattern Formation → Body Plan → Bilateral Symmetry → Adult Form) → Importance (Natural Selection → Optimal Function → Tissue Repair Mechanisms)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • Derived from “morphology” and “generation” 🔴
  • Involves pattern formation, body plan establishment, and mirror-image symmetry in musculoskeletal structures 🔴
  • Key for tissue engineering as it incorporates natural repair mechanisms for damaged tissues 🟢
  • Considers cell origins for organ formation 🟢
  • Focuses on cell-environment interactions (ECM and neighboring cells) 🔵
  • Involves growth factors and their interrelationships in organ formation 🟢
  • Examines mechanisms inducing undifferentiated precursors to organ-specific cells 🔵
  • Result of natural selection over millions of years for optimal adaptation 🟢
  • Essential for repairing non-self-repairing tissues 🔵
  • Human embryo adopts planar morphology during gastrulation 🟢
  • Primitive streak forms via delamination of mesodermal cells 🔴
  • Nodal signaling creates morphogenic gradients 🟢

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Confusing morphogenesis with simple cell division; trap: Questions asking to differentiate from cleavage (morphogenesis involves pattern and symmetry, not just mass increase)
  • Overlooking importance in tissue engineering; mistake: Assuming it’s only embryonic, ignore repair applications
  • Misidentifying morphogenic gradients as uniform; trap: Diagrams without distance-dependent concentration

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • MORPH: Millions Of Years Refine Patterns Holistically (for natural selection importance)
  • Things to Consider: O-I-G-M (Origin of cells, Interaction with environment, Growth factors, Mechanisms of specialization)

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

What is morphogenesis and why is it important in tissue engineering?

Morphogenesis is the developmental cascade of pattern formation, body plan establishment, and bilateral symmetry culminating in adult form; it’s vital as tissue formation results from natural selection for optimal function, aiding incorporation of repair mechanisms in engineering non-self-repairing tissues.

Explain nodal signaling in embryonic disk.

Nodal (BMP4 family) is expressed proximally in epiblast, localizes to node, creating a descending gradient where highest levels are at node, decreasing with distance to form morphogenic gradient.

Topic 2 – Embryonic Development Stages

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Embryonic development progresses from zygote to gastrulation, establishing germ layers for organ formation.
Mind map: Zygote → Cleavage (Blastomeres → Morula) → Compaction → Blastocyst (Trophectoderm + Inner Cell Mass) → Gastrulation (Primitive Streak → Germ Layers: Ectoderm/Mesoderm/Endoderm)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • Zygote: Fertilized oocyte 🔴
  • Cleavage: Divisions without growth, increases cell number, blastomeres totipotent 🟢
  • Morula: 8-cell stage, first differentiation, compaction 🔴
  • Blastocyst: Forms prior to implantation, inner cell mass becomes epiblast 🟢
  • Gastrulation: Establishes 3 germ layers (ectoderm, endoderm, mesoderm) 🔵
  • Human embryo: Planar morphology, amniotic cavity, yolk sac 🔴
  • Primitive endoderm proliferates to form yolk sac 🟢
  • Extraembryonic membranes: Amnion (fluid environment), yolk sac (RBC formation) 🟢
  • Implantation in uterine wall after blastocyst stage 🔴
  • First differentiation at morula stage 🔵
  • Trophectoderm mediates implantation and placenta formation 🟢
  • Umbilical cord connects embryo to placenta 🔴

Key Formulas / Diagrams (use code blocks)

Comparison Table (“X vs Y” in proper Markdown table)

StagePre-Gastrulation (X)Gastrulation (Y)
StructureZygote to blastocyst, sphericalPlanar disk, primitive streak
DifferentiationTotipotent to pluripotent3 germ layers established
Key EventCleavage, compactionSpecification of ecto/endo/meso
OutcomeImplantation prepAdult organ precursors

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Mixing morula and blastocyst; trap: Questions on cell potency (morula blastomeres totipotent, blastocyst pluripotent)
  • Forgetting extraembryonic roles; mistake: Assuming all structures embryonic
  • Incorrect sequence; trap: Timeline diagrams with wrong order

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • Z-C-M-B-G: Zygote-Cleavage-Morula-Blastocyst-Gastrulation
  • Germ Layers: Every Monday Evening (Ectoderm-Mesoderm-Endoderm)

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

Describe cleavage and its outcome.

Cleavage: Zygote divisions without growth, producing totipotent blastomeres; increases cell number without mass, leading to morula.

What occurs during gastrulation?

Subsequent steps establish proper structure for 3 germ layers (ectoderm, endoderm, mesoderm) via primitive streak formation.

Topic 3 – Germ Layers and Tissue Origins

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Germ layers (ectoderm, mesoderm, endoderm) originate during gastrulation and give rise to specific tissues and organs.
Mind map: Gastrulation → Ectoderm (Skin/Nervous System) → Mesoderm (Muscles/Connective Tissue) → Endoderm (Digestive/Respiratory Organs)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • Ectoderm: Skin epidermis, hair, nails, sweat glands, nervous system, retina, lens, cornea, inner ear 🔴
  • Mesoderm: All muscles, dermis, connective tissue, bone, cartilage, blood vessels, kidneys, reproductive organs 🟢
  • Endoderm: Lining of digestive tract, trachea, bronchi, lungs, liver, pancreas, thyroid, parathyroid, thymus, urinary bladder 🔴
  • Origin from primitive streak and node during gastrulation 🟢
  • Node as organizing center 🔵
  • Morphogenic gradient via Nodal signaling 🟢
  • Primitive streak thickens ectoderm midline via mesoderm delamination 🔴
  • Three definitive germ layers construct adult organism 🟢
  • Trophoblast contiguous with ectoderm as trophectoderm 🔴
  • Yolk sac from primitive endoderm 🟢
  • Amniotic cavity between epiblast and trophoblast 🔴
  • Human development video illustrates stages 🟢

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Misassigning tissues; trap: Kidney from endoderm (actually mesoderm)
  • Confusing primitive vs definitive layers; mistake: Primitive endoderm as definitive
  • Ignoring mesoderm’s broad role; trap: Questions on blood vessels (mesoderm)

  • Ecto: External (Skin/Eyes/Nerves)
  • Meso: Middle (Muscles/Bones/Blood)
  • Endo: Internal (Guts/Glands/Lungs)

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

List tissues from mesoderm.

All muscles, dermis of skin, connective tissue, bone, cartilage, blood vessels, kidneys, reproductive organs.

What is the role of the node in germ layer formation?

Anterior-most tip of primitive streak, functions as organizing center for morphogenic gradients.

Topic 4 – Epithelial-Mesenchymal Transition (EMT)

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

EMT/MET involves cellular changes in division, shape, migration, growth, and secretion for tissue morphogenesis.
Mind map: EMT → Loss of Cell-Cell Adhesion → Gain Cell-ECM Adhesion → Polarity Change → Motility/Invasion → Examples (Condensation/Shape Change)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • Epithelial: Sheets/tubes, tightly connected, polarity 🔴
  • Mesenchymal: Unconnected, independent, no polarity 🟢
  • EMT accompanies morphogenesis with changes in division direction/number 🔵
  • Cell shape changes (e.g., cobblestone to acini in MCF-10A) 🟢
  • Cell migration/movement 🔴
  • Cell growth/death 🟢
  • Secretion changes 🔵
  • Example: Vascular mesenchymal cell aggregation (condensation) 🔴
  • MCF-10A on Matrigel forms acini 🟢
  • Lumen formation via cell death in acini 🔴
  • Polarity dependent on cadherins 🟢
  • Occurs in wound healing for alignment 🔵

Comparison Table (“X vs Y” in proper Markdown table)

ProcessEMT (X)MET (Y)
ChangeEpithelial to mesenchymalMesenchymal to epithelial
AdhesionLose cell-cell, gain cell-ECMGain cell-cell, lose cell-ECM
PolarityLose apical-basalGain apical-basal

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Confusing EMT with apoptosis; trap: Lumen formation questions (via death, not just hollowing)
  • Overlooking examples; mistake: Assuming all cells undergo EMT equally
  • Misidentifying polarity role; trap: Cadherin dependency in alignment

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • EMT: Every Migration Transforms (Division/Shape/Movement/Growth/Secretion)
  • 5 Changes: Di-Sha-Mi-Gro-Se (Direction/Shape/Migration/Growth/Secretion)

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

Describe EMT changes.

  1. Direction/number of divisions; 2. Shape changes; 3. Movement/migration; 4. Growth/death; 5. Membrane/secretion changes.

Explain MCF-10A example.

At confluence, cobblestone; in 3D Matrigel, proliferate to clusters then acini with polarized outer layer and inner cell death for lumen.

Topic 5 – Blood Vessel Development

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Blood vessels form via vasculogenesis and angiogenesis to meet embryonic needs by third week.
Mind map: Vasculogenesis (In Situ Endothelial Formation → Network → Stabilization) → Angiogenesis (Sprouting from Existing) → Structure (Adventitia/Media/Intima) → Lumen (Hollowing/Cord)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • Starts by third gestational week due to diffusion limits 🔴
  • Two processes: Vasculogenesis (in situ in non-vascularized tissue) 🟢
  • Angiogenesis (new vessels from existing) 🔵
  • Vessel walls: Adventitia, media, intima (capillaries thin endothelium) 🟢
  • Linked to heart development 🔴
  • Initial network: Veins/arteries + plexus 🟢
  • Growth factors stabilize walls, recruit pericytes/SMCs 🔵
  • Blood flow essential for proper development 🔴
  • Endothelial tube on fibronectin lines (30μm wide) 🟢
  • Lumen models: Cell hollowing (vacuoles coalesce) or cord hollowing (shape changes) 🔴
  • Cord hollowing more feasible for vasculogenesis 🟢
  • Luminal cell death alternative mechanism 🔵

Key Formulas / Diagrams (use code blocks)

Comparison Table (“X vs Y” in proper Markdown table)

MechanismIn situ endothelial formationSprouting/branching

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Confusing vasculogenesis with angiogenesis; trap: Initial vs expansion questions
  • Ignoring flow role; mistake: Assuming vessels develop without hemodynamics
  • Wrong lumen model; trap: Assuming only hollowing (evidence for cord)

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • V-A: Vasculogenesis (Virgin Areas) vs Angiogenesis (Add to Existing)
  • Lumen: Hollow Cords Vacate (Hollowing/Cord/Vacuoles)

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

Differentiate vasculogenesis and angiogenesis.

Vasculogenesis: In situ endothelial formation in non-vascularized tissue, initial network; Angiogenesis: New endothelium from existing vessels.

Describe lumen formation models.

Cell hollowing: Vacuoles coalesce and interconnect; Cord hollowing: Packed cells change shape, break junctions for extracellular lumen.

Topic 6 – Branching Morphogenesis

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Branching morphogenesis creates complex structures like lungs via inductive chemicals and inhibitors.
Mind map: Traditional Model (FGF10 Induce → Shh/BMP4 Inhibit) → Types (Domain/Orthogonal/Planar) → Reaction-Diffusion (Activator/Inhibitor → Patterns)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • Traditional: FGF10 inductive, Shh/BMP4/Sprouty inhibit for branching 🔴
  • Lung types: Domain, orthogonal, planar 🟢
  • Reaction-diffusion: Turing’s activator-inhibitor system 🔵
  • Activator: BMP-2 (postulated); Inhibitor: MGP 🟢
  • Equations involve diffusion, reaction, chemotaxis 🔴
  • Applications: Pigment distribution, hair follicles 🟢
  • Domain branching: Low inhibitor consumption 🔵
  • Orthogonal: High consumption 🔴
  • Planar: With boundary effect 🟢
  • Simulation matches lung anatomy 🔵
  • Alan Turing: Mathematical biology pioneer 🔴
  • System: Pair activator-inhibitor with cells 🟢

  • Misapplying equations; mistake: Forgetting cell term
  • Confusing branch types; trap: Lung anatomy diagrams

  • Branch Types: DOP (Domain-Orthogonal-Planar)
  • Turing: Turing’s Universal Reaction Inhibits Natural Growth (for activator-inhibitor)

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

Explain traditional branching model.

FGF10 as inductive chemical; Shh, BMP4, Sprouty inhibit FGF10, leading to localized branching.

Describe reaction-diffusion in branching.

Activator (BMP-2) and inhibitor (MGP) system with cells; diffusion/reaction create patterns like lung branches.

Topic 7 – Left-Right Asymmetry

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Left-right asymmetry arises from cellular chirality and cilia to establish organ sidedness.
Mind map: LR Asymmetry → Cilia (Node Flow → Gradient) → Planar Chirality (Epithelium → Rotation) → Examples (Heart/Fiber → Twins/Mirror Imaging)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • Prominent from microscopic to macroscopic levels 🔴
  • Cilia at node generate leftward flow for sidedness 🟢
  • Flow sets morphogen gradient or activation 🔵
  • Planar cell polarity orients cilia 🔴
  • Not solely cilia; early twins show mirror imaging 🟢
  • Single cell chirality: BDM induces counter-clockwise torsion 🔵
  • Hindgut rotation driven by epithelial planar chirality 🔴
  • Vascular mesenchymal cells form chiral structures on patterns 🟢
  • Skeletal myogenesis: Chiral orientation before fusion 🔵
  • Myotubes on wide grooves show intense contractions 🔴
  • Engineering: Actin striation in myotubes 🟢
  • Twins: Book-ending defects suggest pre-cilia chirality 🔵

How do cilia establish LR asymmetry?

Node cilia generate leftward fluid flow, setting extracellular morphogen gradient or differential activation.

Explain single cell chirality in asymmetry.

BDM treatment post-fertilization induces counter-clockwise torsion in zygotes, contributing to LR before cilia.

Topic 8 – Cell Adhesion Molecules

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Cell adhesion via CAMs like cadherins, integrins, selectins mediates cell-cell/matrix interactions for tissue formation.
Mind map: Adhesion → Families (Cadherins → Integrins → Selectins → Ig CAMs) → Types (Homotypic/Heterotypic) → Functions (Junctions/Signaling/Rolling)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • Four families: Cadherins (Ca-dependent, cell-cell), Ig CAMs (Ca-independent), Integrins (cell-matrix), Selectins (leukocyte rolling) 🔴
  • Homotypic: Same molecules; Heterotypic: Different 🟢
  • Cadherins: E (epithelial), N (neural), P (placenta); Ca-binding domain 🔵
  • Integrins: αβ heterodimers, 18α/8β, mechanotransducers, bidirectional signaling 🔴
  • Selectins: L (leukocytes), E (ECs), P (platelets/ECs); Ca-dependent carbohydrate-binding 🟢
  • Focal adhesions: Concentrated sites via integrins 🔵
  • In wound healing: Cadherin-dependent polarity 🔴
  • Leukocyte rolling: Selectin-mediated transient adhesion 🟢
  • Extravasation: Selectin arrest + integrin adhesion 🔵
  • Lung-on-chip: Reconstitutes alveolar-capillary interface 🔴
  • Inflammation: Neutrophil extravasation via cytokines/bacteria 🟢
  • Cell-cell vs cell-substrate adhesion crucial for engineering 🔵

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Confusing cadherins with integrins; trap: Binding types (cell-cell vs matrix)
  • Forgetting Ca role; mistake: Cadherins work without Ca (dimer fails)
  • Misunderstanding selectin rolling; trap: Permanent vs transient adhesion

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • CAM Families: C-I-S-I (Cadherins-Integrins-Selectins-Ig)
  • Integrin: Inside-Out, Outside-In (Bidirectional signaling)

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

Compare cadherins and integrins.

Cadherins: Ca-dependent cell-cell adhesion, homotypic, e.g., E-cadherin; Integrins: Cell-matrix, αβ, mechanotransducers, bidirectional.

Describe selectin-mediated rolling.

Transient adhesion under flow; L-selectin on leukocytes binds PSGL-1 on ECs, leading to arrest then integrin-mediated transmigration.

Topic 9 – Extracellular Matrix (ECM)

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

ECM provides structural support and signaling via proteins, GAGs, and glycoproteins surrounding cells.
Mind map: ECM → Components (Proteins → GAGs → Glycoproteins) → Basal Lamina (Sheet 60-120nm) → Types (Collagen/Laminin/Elastin/Fibronectin)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • 3D network of fibers in hydrated gel 🔴
  • Secreted locally by cells, assembles scaffold for attachment/proliferation 🟢
  • Cells modify via enzymes/load 🔵
  • Components: Proteins (collagen/elastin/laminin), GAGs (chondroitin/heparin), Proteoglycans (fibrinogen) 🔴
  • Basal lamina: 60-120nm sheet, surrounds epithelia, important in repair/embryogenesis 🟢
  • Basement membrane: Basal lamina + collagen VI + reticular tissue 🔵
  • Collagen: Main strength, 25-35% body protein, triple helix (Type I: 2α1+1α2) 🔴
  • Type IV: In basal lamina, 7S/middle/NC1 domains, forms hexamers/tetramers 🟢
  • Laminin: Networks in basal lamina, binds cells/integrins, for stem cell culture 🔵
  • Elastin: Elasticity, stretches 5x, interwoven with collagen 🔴
  • Fibronectin: Binds integrins/collagen/fibrin, soluble (plasma) or insoluble (cellular) 🟢
  • Wrinkle: Broken elastin replaced by collagen, looser/stiffer skin 🔵

  • Confusing basal lamina with basement; trap: Thickness/composition (lamina 60-120nm, basement includes collagen layer)
  • Misidentifying collagen types; mistake: Type IV as fibrillar (actually network-forming)
  • Overlooking fibronectin types; trap: Soluble vs insoluble roles

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • ECM: Extra Cellular Material (Proteins-GAGs-Glycoproteins)
  • Collagen Types: I Fibril, IV Basal (1-Fiber, 4-Foundation)

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

What is ECM composition?

Proteins (collagen, elastin, laminin), GAGs (chondroitin, heparin), glycoproteins/proteoglycans (fibronectin, fibrinogen).

Describe Type IV collagen structure.

Alpha-chains with 7S, triple-helical, NC1 domains; two assemble to NC1 hexamer, four to 7S tetramer.

15-Minute Mock Exam

  1. Explain the importance of morphogenesis in tissue engineering.

    It’s a process from natural selection for optimal tissue function; incorporating mechanisms aids repair of non-self-repairing tissues.

  2. Describe gastrulation process.

    Establishes 3 germ layers (ecto/endo/meso) via primitive streak in planar embryo with amniotic cavity and yolk sac.

  3. Compare epithelial and mesenchymal cells.

    Epithelial: Tightly connected sheets, polar; Mesenchymal: Loose independent units, non-polar.

  4. What are the two lumen formation models?

    Cell hollowing (vacuoles coalesce); Cord hollowing (shape changes break junctions).

  5. Explain reaction-diffusion in branching.

    Activator-inhibitor pair (e.g., BMP-2/MGP) with diffusion/reaction creates patterns like branches.

  6. How is LR asymmetry established beyond cilia?

    Single cell chirality (torsion) and planar chirality (epithelium rotation) contribute, as seen in twins.

  7. Differentiate cadherins and selectins.

    Cadherins: Ca-dependent cell-cell junctions; Selectins: Ca-dependent carbohydrate-binding for leukocyte rolling.

  8. What is ECM and its components?

    Non-cellular network for support/signaling; proteins (collagen), GAGs, glycoproteins (fibronectin).
