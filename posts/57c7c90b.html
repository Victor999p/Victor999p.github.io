BME2104 Tissue Engineering – 2

Lecture Overview

  • Top 8 topics that appear every year: Principles of Tissue Engineering, Cell Biology and Types, Stem Cells and Differentiation, Biomaterials and Biocompatibility, Engineering Strategies and Bioreactors, Tissue Regeneration (e.g., Skin, Bone, Cartilage), Ethical and Immune Issues, Clinical Applications

  • 顏色標記：🔴 死背 | 🟢 要明白 | 🔵 要識用

主題1 – 組織工程基本概念（Tissue Engineering Paradigm）

大圖景

組織工程用「細胞 + 生物材料 + 生化因子 + 物理刺激」四樣嘢一齊整返受損組織。

必記重點（最多12點）

  • 🔴 四樣必備：細胞、生物相容材料、生化因子（例如生長因子）、物理因子（例如循環機械負荷）
  • 🟢 目標：修補或者取代壞咗嘅組織
  • 🔵 典型流程：搵細胞 → 擴增 → 種落支架 → 植入體內
  • 🟢 Blitterswijk 2008經典圖：細胞 + 支架 + 信號 = 新組織

常見錯處同陷阱

  • 答漏咗「物理因子」只講生長因子 → 扣分
  • 以為只要有細胞同支架就夠 → 唔記得生化同物理刺激

主題2 – 分化細胞（Differentiated Cells）

大圖景

分化細胞各有專長，但搵唔到多、繁殖差、取材會痛 → 唔係組織工程首選。

必記重點

  • 🔴 角質細胞（Keratinocyte）：皮膚屏障，產生角蛋白
  • 🔴 紅血球：運氧，產生血紅蛋白
  • 🔴 軟骨細胞（Chondrocyte）：支撐，產生II型膠原
  • 🔴 肝細胞（Hepatocyte）：超多功能，產生白蛋白
  • 🟢 四大缺點：來源少、取材痛、繁殖差、已經「專一化」
  • 🔵 同幹細胞比：幹細胞未決定做乜，先聽環境信號

比較表

分化細胞 vs 幹細胞分化細胞幹細胞
來源數量好少少但可擴增
繁殖能力差超強
功能已經鎖死未鎖，可變

主題3 – 細胞培養同細胞系（Cell Lines）

大圖景

Primary culture → 第一次傳代之後就叫cell line → 再分finite同immortalized（例如HeLa）。

必記重點

  • 🔴 HeLa係最舊、最常用嘅人類永生化細胞系（1951年，Henrietta Lacks子宮頸癌）
  • 🟢 HeLa自然轉化，繁殖超強，全球已生咗20噸！
  • 🔵 問題：HeLa污染其他細胞系好常見
  • 🔴 Immortalized要恢復端粒酶（telomerase）活性

陷阱

  • 記錯HeLa係病毒感染 → 其實係自然轉化

主題4 – 幹細胞基本概念（Stem Cells Fundamentals）

大圖景

幹細胞有兩大特徵：自我更新（self-renewal）＋分化能力（differentiation），合稱「stemness」。

必記重點

  • 🔴 自我更新可分對稱（symmetric）同不對稱（asymmetric）
  • 🔵 不對稱分兩種：
    • Intrinsic：細胞內部蛋白/RNAs分佈唔平均
    • Extrinsic：靠niche位置同信號
  • 🟢 分化路徑：幹細胞 → 中間放大細胞（progenitor）→ 好多分化細胞
  • 🔴 骨髓入面10,000–15,000個先有1個幹細胞，但每日產生幾十億血細胞！

記憶口訣

「對稱加減，不對稱一加一減」

主題5 – 胚胎幹細胞（Embryonic Stem Cells, ESCs）

大圖景

由囊胚內細胞團搵出，全能性最高，但有道德爭議同排斥問題。

必記重點

  • 🔴 分離方法：immunosurgery（用抗體+補體殺外層滋養層）
  • 🟢 人類ESC要種喺小鼠feeder layer + bFGF（小鼠用LIF就夠）
  • 🔴 標記物：Oct4、ALP、SSEA-3/4、高端粒酶
  • 🟢 驗證全能性：
    • 體外 → 做embryoid body（三胚層）
    • 體內 → 打入免疫缺陷鼠生teratoma（三胚層腫瘤）
  • 🔵 應用：已成功分化神經元、心肌、肝細胞、胰島β細胞等

陷阱

  • 以為人類ESC都用LIF → 錯！人類要bFGF

主題6 – 成人幹細胞（Adult Stem Cells）

大圖景

成人身上都有幹細胞（例如骨髓、脂肪、臍帶血），數量少但可自體用，無道德問題。

必記重點

  • 🔴 兩大類：
    • HSC（造血幹細胞）：CD34+、CD38-
    • MSC（間充質幹細胞）：STRO-1+、CD34-
  • 🟢 來源：骨髓（痛）、脂肪（抽脂）、臍帶血、智慧齒
  • 🔵 Niche：HSC靠骨面或血管壁；MSC多數喺血管旁（perivascular）

主題7 – 幹細胞可塑性（Plasticity）

大圖景

以前以為成人幹細胞只可以變同一個germ layer嘅細胞，而家發現可以跨界（transdifferentiation）。

必記重點

  • 🟢 例子：血管幹細胞變骨細胞 → 主動脈硬化同鈣化可能同幹細胞有關

主題8 – 誘導多能幹細胞（iPSCs）

大圖景

用4個基因把成人細胞「倒帶」返去似ESC，山中伸彌2012諾貝爾獎。

必記重點

  • 🔴 4個因子：Oct4、Sox2、c-Myc、Klf4（簡稱OSCK）
  • 🟢 好處：自體、無道德問題、數量無限
  • 🔵 壞處：成功率0.01–0.1%、有致癌風險（c-Myc）、病毒插入突變
  • 🟢 第二代用Nanog篩選，先至做到chimera mouse
  • 🔴 最新（2022）：已做到體外合成胚胎（post-gastrulation synthetic embryos）

Topic 1 – Tissue Engineering Paradigm

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Tissue engineering integrates cells, biocompatible materials, biochemical factors, and biophysical cues to regenerate damaged tissues and organs.
Mind map:
Tissue Engineering → Cells (source) → Materials (scaffolds) → Biochemical Factors (growth factors) → Biophysical Factors (mechanical loading) → Applications (regeneration/implants)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Core components: cells, biocompatible materials, biochemical factors (e.g., growth factors), biophysical factors (e.g., cyclic mechanical loading).
  • 🟢 Aims to repair or replace damaged tissues using engineered constructs.
  • 🔵 Involves cell expansion, seeding on matrices, and implantation.
  • 🟢 Blends biology and engineering for clinical implementation.
  • 🔴 Examples include skin, bone, and cartilage regeneration.
  • 🟢 Biochemical factors influence cell growth and differentiation.
  • 🔵 Biophysical factors like loading simulate natural environments.
  • 🟢 Paradigm from Blitterswijk (2008): cells + scaffolds + signals.
  • 🔴 Focuses on biocompatibility to avoid immune rejection.
  • 🟢 Multidisciplinary: biology, materials science, engineering.

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Mistaking biochemical factors for only growth factors (trap: ignore cytokines or hormones).
  • Overlooking biophysical cues like mechanical loading in construct design.
  • Assuming all materials are biocompatible without testing for degradation or toxicity.

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • “Cells Make Bio-Bio Products” (Cells, Materials, Biochemical, Biophysical).

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

What are the four key components of the tissue engineering paradigm?

Cells, biocompatible materials, biochemical factors (e.g., growth factors), and biophysical factors (e.g., cyclic mechanical loading).

Describe a clinical application of tissue engineering.

Skin regeneration using cell-seeded scaffolds for burn victims.

Topic 2 – Cell Types and Differentiated Cells

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Differentiated cells perform specific functions in tissues but have limitations in availability and proliferation for engineering applications.
Mind map:
Cell Types → Function (e.g., Keratinocyte: barrier) → Biochemicals (e.g., keratin) → Limitations → Availability → Morbidity → Proliferation → Commitment

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Keratinocyte: mechanical barrier, produces keratin.
  • 🔴 Red blood cell: oxygen transport, hemoglobin.
  • 🔴 Chondrocyte: physical support, Type II collagen.
  • 🔴 Hepatocyte: multiple functions, albumin.
  • 🟢 Limitations: limited autologous availability, harvest morbidity.
  • 🟢 Limited proliferative and biosynthetic activity.
  • 🔵 Committed to specific functions unlike stem cells.
  • 🟢 Used in primary cultures but hard to expand.
  • 🔴 Examples: Myocyte for contraction (actin/myosin).
  • 🟢 Neuron: electrical impulses, neurotransmitters.
  • 🔵 B lymphocyte: antibody synthesis, immunoglobulins.

Key Formulas / Diagrams (use code blocks)

Comparison Table (“X vs Y” in proper Markdown table)

Primary vs Differentiated CellsPrimary CellsDifferentiated Cells
SourceDirectly from tissueCommitted post-differentiation
ProliferationLimited passagesVery limited
FunctionRetain originalSpecific, fixed
Use in TEAuthentic but scarceFunctional but hard to harvest

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Confusing cell function with biochemical produced (trap: hepatocyte makes albumin, not insulin).
  • Ignoring morbidity in harvesting differentiated cells.
  • Assuming differentiated cells can dedifferentiate easily.

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • “KRH LBC PMI N H” (Keratinocyte-Red-Hemoglobin-Lens-B lymphocyte-Chondrocyte-Pancreatic-Myocyte-Neuron-Hepatocyte).

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

List limitations of using differentiated cells in tissue engineering.

Limited availability, harvest morbidity, limited proliferation, committed function.

What is the function of a chondrocyte?

Physical support, produces Type II collagen.

Topic 3 – Cell Cultures and Cell Lines

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Cell cultures maintain isolated cells for study and engineering, with cell lines providing immortalized options like HeLa for scalable use.
Mind map:
Cell Culture → Primary (from tissue) → Cell Line (subculture) → Finite (limited) → Immortalized (transformed) → HeLa (cervical cancer origin)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Primary culture: cells isolated from tissue until confluence.
  • 🟢 Cell line: after first subculture, finite or immortalized.
  • 🔴 Finite: limited proliferative capability.
  • 🟢 Immortalized: transformation (spontaneous/induced), restores telomerase.
  • 🔴 HeLa: oldest human line from Henrietta Lacks (1951), cervical cancer.
  • 🟢 HeLa: spontaneous immortalization, highly proliferative.
  • 🔵 Risk: HeLa contamination of other lines due to dominance.
  • 🟢 Estimated 20 tons of HeLa cells grown worldwide.
  • 🔴 Transformation: chemical, viral, or spontaneous.
  • 🟢 Telomeres shorten in duplication; telomerase prevents this.

Key Formulas / Diagrams (use code blocks)

Comparison Table (“X vs Y” in proper Markdown table)

Finite vs Immortalized Cell LinesFiniteImmortalized
ProliferationLimitedUnlimited
TransformationNoneSpontaneous/induced
UseAuthentic phenotypeScalable, but altered
ExampleNormal fibroblastsHeLa cells

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Thinking primary cultures are immortal (trap: they become cell lines post-subculture).
  • Forgetting HeLa’s contamination risk in labs.
  • Confusing telomerase role (shortens telomeres without it).

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • “Primary First, Line After Sub” (Primary culture to cell line).

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

What defines a cell line?

Primary culture after first subculture.

Describe HeLa cells.

Immortalized from cervical cancer, highly proliferative, contamination risk.

Topic 4 – Stem Cells Fundamentals

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Stem cells self-renew and differentiate, defined by stemness, with types including embryonic and adult for tissue regeneration.
Mind map:
Stem Cells → Stemness (Self-Renew + Differentiate) → Types (Embryonic → Flexible; Adult → Committed) → Self-Renewal (Symmetric/Asymmetric) → Differentiation (Progenitors → Specialized)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Definition: self-renewal and differentiation ability.
  • 🟢 Stemness: properties of renewal and forming other types.
  • 🔴 Types: embryonic (pluripotent, moral issues) and adult (multipotent, low numbers).
  • 🟢 Self-renewal: maintains stem phenotype via symmetric/asymmetric division.
  • 🔵 Asymmetric: one stem, one differentiated daughter.
  • 🟢 Intrinsic asymmetry: polarity regulators in cytoplasm.
  • 🔴 Extrinsic: spindle orientation in niche.
  • 🟢 Differentiation: via progenitors (transit amplifying cells).
  • 🔵 Example: bone marrow stem cells produce billions of blood cells daily.
  • 🟢 De-differentiation: loss of phenotype (e.g., chondrocytes in vitro).
  • 🔴 Transdifferentiation: switch to different cell type.
  • 🟢 Potency: totipotent (all cells + extraembryonic), pluripotent (3 germ layers).

Key Formulas / Diagrams (use code blocks)

Comparison Table (“X vs Y” in proper Markdown table)

Symmetric vs Asymmetric DivisionSymmetricAsymmetric
OutcomeTwo stems or two differentiatedOne stem, one differentiated
BalanceIncreases/decreases stem poolMaintains stem pool
MechanismEqual distributionIntrinsic/extrinsic asymmetry

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Confusing self-renewal with proliferation (trap: renewal maintains stemness).
  • Mixing intrinsic (internal) vs extrinsic (niche) asymmetry.
  • Assuming differentiation is irreversible (forget de-/transdifferentiation).

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • “Self-Renew Differentiate” (SRD for stemness).

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

What defines stemness?

Ability to self-renew and differentiate.

Explain asymmetric division.

One daughter remains stem; other differentiates, via intrinsic or extrinsic mechanisms.

Topic 5 – Embryonic Stem Cells

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Embryonic stem cells (ESCs) are pluripotent from early embryos, cultured on feeders for undifferentiated growth but challenging to maintain.
Mind map:
ESCs → Isolation (Blastocyst → Immunosurgery) → Culture (Feeder Cells + Factors) → Markers (Oct4, ALP) → Applications (Neurons, Cardiomyocytes) → Concerns (Rejection, Tumors)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • 🔴 From inner cell mass of blastocyst.
  • 🟢 Isolation: immunosurgery removes trophoblasts.
  • 🔴 Culture: on mouse fibroblasts (irradiated), with bFGF for humans.
  • 🟢 Difficult: overgrowth leads to differentiation; dissociate often.
  • 🔵 Markers: Oct4, ALP, SSEA-3/4 (human), high telomerase.
  • 🟢 Embryoid bodies: aggregates form 3 germ layers.
  • 🔴 In vivo: teratoma formation confirms pluripotency.
  • 🟢 Applications: neurons, cardiomyocytes, hepatocytes.
  • 🔵 Concerns: immune rejection, purify differentiated, remove pluripotent.
  • 🟢 Optimization: closed-loop for cocktails using Oct4.
  • 🔴 Mouse: LIF instead of feeders.
  • 🟢 Animal-free desired for clinical use.

Key Formulas / Diagrams (use code blocks)

Comparison Table (“X vs Y” in proper Markdown table)

Mouse vs Human ESCsMouseHuman
Growth FactorLIFbFGF
DissociationTrypsinMechanical
Feeder CellsOptional with LIFRequired

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Thinking LIF works for human ESCs (trap: only mouse).
  • Forgetting teratoma as pluripotency test.
  • Ignoring ethical concerns with embryonic sources.

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • “Oct ALP SSEA Telo EB” (Markers: Oct4, ALP, SSEA, Telomerase, Embryoid Bodies).

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

How are ESCs isolated?

Immunosurgery on blastocyst to remove trophoblasts, leaving inner cell mass.

List ESCs markers.

Oct4, alkaline phosphatase, SSEA-3/4, high telomerase.

Topic 6 – Adult Stem Cells

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

Adult stem cells maintain tissue homeostasis, found in low numbers in tissues like bone marrow, with multipotency for regeneration.
Mind map:
Adult SC → Types (HSC, MSC) → Isolation (Bone Marrow, Cord Blood) → Characterization (Markers like CD34) → Niche (Perivascular) → Plasticity (Transdifferentiation)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Role: replace lost cells, maintain homeostasis.
  • 🟢 Sources: bone marrow, cord blood, adipose, amniotic fluid.
  • 🔴 HSCs: produce blood cells, markers CD34+, CD38-.
  • 🟢 MSCs: bone, cartilage, fat; markers STRO-1+, CD34-.
  • 🔵 Isolation: invasive (bone marrow) or cytokines for blood.
  • 🟢 Niche: microenvironment for quiescence/activity.
  • 🔴 Deterministic: spatial constraints for asymmetry.
  • 🟢 Stochastic: switch between division modes.
  • 🔵 Plasticity: differentiate across lineages.
  • 🟢 First discovered: 1960s in bone marrow (HSC/MSC).
  • 🔴 Vascular SC: osteogenic in atherosclerosis.
  • 🟢 Low numbers: e.g., 1 in 10,000-15,000 in marrow.

Comparison Table (“X vs Y” in proper Markdown table)

DifferentiationBlood cellsBone, cartilage, fat
MarkersCD34+, CD38-STRO-1+, CD34-

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Assuming adult SC only differentiate in own tissue (trap: plasticity allows transdifferentiation).
  • Confusing HSC (blood) with MSC (mesenchymal).
  • Forgetting niche role in maintaining stemness.

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • “Hemo Meso Adult” (HSC, MSC in adult tissues).

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

What is the role of adult stem cells?

Maintain homeostasis, replace lost cells via progenitors.

Compare HSCs and MSCs.

HSCs for blood; MSCs for connective tissues.

Topic 7 – Stem Cell Niche and Plasticity

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

The stem cell niche provides a protective environment balancing renewal and differentiation, while plasticity allows unexpected lineage switches.
Mind map:
Niche → Microenvironment → Deterministic (Spatial) vs Stochastic (Switch) → HSC (Bone/Vessels) → MSC (Perivascular) → Plasticity (Cross-Lineage)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Niche: shelters stem cells, balances quiescence/activity.
  • 🟢 Deterministic: asymmetric division by constraints.
  • 🔴 Stochastic: random symmetric/asymmetric switch.
  • 🟢 HSC niche: bone surfaces or sinusoids.
  • 🔵 MSC niche: perivascular, interactions via adhesion/ECM.
  • 🟢 Plasticity: adult SC differentiate beyond tissue (e.g., across germ layers).
  • 🔴 Vascular SC: from bovine artery, form bone in sclerosis.
  • 🟢 Hypothesis: stem cells cause aortic calcification.
  • 🔵 New research: transdifferentiation possible.
  • 🟢 Low frequency but significant for TE.

Key Formulas / Diagrams (use code blocks)

Comparison Table (“X vs Y” in proper Markdown table)

Deterministic vs Stochastic NicheDeterministicStochastic
DivisionAlways asymmetricSwitches modes
ControlSpatial/functional constraintsProbabilistic
OutcomeRetained in nicheVariable pool size

Common Mistakes & Examiner Traps

  • Assuming all niches are deterministic (trap: MSCs more stochastic).
  • Underestimating plasticity in adult SC.
  • Confusing niche with ECM alone.

Mnemonics / Memory Hooks

  • “Det Sto Niche” (Deterministic Stochastic).

Past Exam Questions + Model Answers (use > blockquote for answers)

Describe stem cell niche models.

Deterministic: spatial retention; Stochastic: random division modes.

What is plasticity?

Ability to differentiate across lineages.

Topic 8 – Induced Pluripotent Stem Cells (iPSCs)

Big Picture (1 sentence + mind map in text)

iPSCs reprogram adult cells to pluripotent state using factors, offering autologous sources without ethical issues of ESCs.
Mind map:
iPSCs → Reprogramming (Oct4, Sox2, c-Myc, Klf4) → Retroviral Delivery → Characterization (Chimera, Teratoma) → Advantages (Unlimited, Autologous) → Limitations (Low Efficiency, Tumors)

Must-Know Facts (max 12 bullet points)

  • 🔴 Reprogram somatic cells to ESC-like.
  • 🟢 Factors: Oct4, Sox2 (pluripotency); c-Myc, Klf4 (phenotype/proliferation).
  • 🔴 Delivered via retrovirus.
  • 🟢 Mouse 2006, human 2007 (Yamanaka, Nobel 2012).
  • 🔵 Characterization: embryoid bodies, teratoma, chimera formation.
  • 🟢 Advantages: from adult tissues, unlimited, no ethics like ESCs.
  • 🔴 Limitations: low throughput (0.01-0.1%), mutations, oncogenes.
  • 🟢 Second gen: Nanog selection, chimeric mice.
  • 🔵 Risk: 20% chimeric mice develop cancer from c-Myc.
  • 🟢 From stomach, liver, skin, etc.
  • 🔴 Latest: synthetic embryos ex utero (2022).
  • 🟢 Autologous but potential rejection.

15-Minute Mock Exam

  1. Describe the tissue engineering paradigm.

    Integrates cells, materials, biochemical and biophysical factors for regeneration.

  2. List three limitations of differentiated cells.

    Limited availability, morbidity, low proliferation.

  3. What defines a cell line?

    Primary culture after subculture.

  4. Explain stem cell self-renewal.

    Division maintaining stem phenotype, symmetric or asymmetric.

  5. How are ESCs cultured?

    On mouse feeders with bFGF for humans.

  6. What is the role of stem cell niche?

    Balances quiescence and differentiation.

  7. Describe iPSC reprogramming.

    Using Oct4, Sox2, c-Myc, Klf4 via retrovirus.

  8. Compare ESCs and iPSCs.

    ESCs from embryos (ethical issues); iPSCs from adults (low efficiency).
